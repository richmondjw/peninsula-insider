April on the Peninsula

Locals will tell you April is the secret best time to visit — and they're not wrong. The summer hordes have retreated to the city, but the weather is often still glorious: cool, crisp mornings giving way to mild, golden afternoons. The vineyards are in full autumn colour, restaurants are less rushed and more attentive, and you can actually get a table at places that were booked out all January.

🍂

Autumn Vineyards

Reds and golds across the vines — particularly beautiful in Red Hill and Main Ridge

🌡️

Weather

Average 14–19°C in April. Mornings crisp, afternoons mild. Occasional showers add to the atmosphere

🦞

Seafood Season

Abalone, crayfish, squid — autumn is one of the best times for peninsula seafood

🍄

Truffle & Mushroom

Late April sees Victorian truffles and wild mushrooms on menus throughout the region

🏪

Quieter Crowd

Post-Easter, the peninsula's tourism pressure eases dramatically — more intimate experiences

♨️

Hot Springs Perfect

The geothermal pools feel even more indulgent against cool autumn air — the contrast is extraordinary

April Holidays at Peninsula Hot Springs
Peninsula Hot Springs, Fingal

The strongest verified April programming on the Peninsula right now. Highlights include guided sound bathing sessions on 4 and 11 April, Bathe in Cinema nights on 9 and 16 April, live music across the holiday period, and Family Body Clay sessions from Friday to Monday at 11am during the holidays (excluding 3 April). Holiday hours are extended, with the Bath House from 5am and the Amphitheatre from 7am over Easter.
