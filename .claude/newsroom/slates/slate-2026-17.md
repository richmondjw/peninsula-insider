## Meeting Notes

Tyler opened with a straightforward brand note: ANZAC Day is the week's civic anchor and the vintage close is the food anchor — both are real Peninsula stories that cannot run elsewhere. He flagged he would be writing the Editor's Letter around the ANZAC theme and asked that the commissioned culture piece not duplicate his frame; Iris confirmed hers would stay at ground level (the organisers, not the meaning).

Margot's sharpest triage moment was on Lucien's first vintage pitch, which came in as a survey of the 2026 cool-climate vintage. She pushed back immediately: angle says nothing. Who are we in the room with? Where are we standing? Lucien reshaped it on the spot — three wineries, final bins, the growers' own read on the year — and it cleared.

One pitch killed: Iris had brought a tourism-adjacent piece on autumn walks for families on the Peninsula — three parks, suggested picnic spots, a gear list. Tyler cut it at the angle question: that runs in every Victorian lifestyle title in April. Iris pulled it without argument; the Arthurs Seat essay came out of the gap.
