## The Angle
A horticultural therapy program run through Red Hill Community Health Centre started as a quiet grief support group and has quietly become the most-attended program on the Peninsula — without any public profile or promotion.

## Subject
**Sandra Pryce**, horticultural therapist, program founder, Red Hill Community Health Centre. Contact: Iris via health centre. **Confirmation required before commissioning is finalised** — Sandra must consent to be profiled and named.

Secondary voices: 2–3 participants (anonymous on request; first names only unless they consent to full attribution).

## Why Now
Autumn is when grief sharpens — shorter days, end of year approaching. The program has just completed its third year and is being considered for state health funding expansion. This is the moment the story surfaces from private to public.

## Structure Sketch
1. **Open with a participant** — specific, unnamed unless consenting. A morning in the garden. What the hands are doing. What they don't say out loud.
2. **Sandra's origin** — how the program started, what it was originally supposed to be, how it grew without being planned
3. **What the research says** — horticultural therapy evidence base, briefly. Not clinical. The writer explains it through Sandra's practice, not the literature.
4. **The question of scale** — funding, growth, whether it survives becoming official. Sandra's ambivalence about expansion.
