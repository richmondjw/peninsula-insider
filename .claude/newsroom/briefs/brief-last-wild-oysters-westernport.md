# Commissioning Brief

**Piece:** The Last Wild Oysters of Westernport
**Section:** Food & Drink
**Writer:** Lucien Faure
**Slot:** Cover

---

## The Angle
A family operation at Merricks North is harvesting Westernport Bay's only remaining wild oysters — and they don't know how many seasons they have left before water quality or regulation ends it.

## Subject
**Tom Kearney**, third-generation shellfish harvester, Merricks North. Contact via Lucien's source list. Pre-existing relationship — warm.

## Why Now
Autumn is peak wild oyster season on Westernport. State marine environment review is underway, with public submissions closing May 15 — the Kearney operation's future may depend on the outcome. This is the last moment to tell this story before the season ends and the review decides.

## Structure Sketch
1. **Open at low tide** — Tom on the flats, hands in cold water, the specific sensory detail of a harvest that has no machine equivalent
2. **The family history** — three generations, what's changed in the bay, the old abundance vs now
3. **The threat** — water quality, regulation, the review underway. Tom's voice on what losing it would mean — not melodramatic, specific
4. **What wild actually means** — flavour, ecosystem, the difference between this and farmed. Lucien's editorial voice comes in here.
