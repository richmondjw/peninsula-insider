Couples Retreats on the Mornington Peninsula

The Peninsula doesn't try to be a resort destination. The best couples' stays here work because they remove planning pressure — the cellar door is next to the bedroom, the vineyard restaurant is downstairs, the thermal springs are a short drive, and the itinerary largely arranges itself once you're in the right place.

Jackalope is the cinematic choice: a design hotel that operates at an event register, best when the trip needs to feel like an occasion. Polperro and Point Leo Estate Villas are more private — self-contained on working estates, with vineyard views and no shared corridors to navigate. Lindenderry runs at a slightly warmer frequency: a boutique hotel on a small vineyard that works for couples who want a room with character rather than spectacle.

For coast-focused weekends, Hotel Sorrento and The Continental are the main-street village hotel options. Sorrento Coastal Retreat suits those who want seclusion close to the ocean.

Frequently asked questions

Most romantic place to stay?

Jackalope (Red Hill) for dramatic design and on-site dining. Polperro Villas and Point Leo Estate Villas for private estate-villa seclusion. Sorrento Coastal Retreat or The Continental for coastal romance with ocean access and back beach walks within reach.

Best for an anniversary?

Jackalope if the trip needs to feel like an event. Lindenderry for a warmer, more personal register. For the most impressive setting in Victoria: Laura at Pt. Leo Estate for dinner (book four months ahead), combined with Point Leo Estate Villas for the stay.

Properties with private baths or outdoor soaking?

Jackalope rooms feature oversized freestanding baths. Polperro and Point Leo Estate Villas have private outdoor spaces. Peninsula Hot Springs Glamping includes access to private thermal pools.
