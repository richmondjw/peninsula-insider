Winery Accommodation on the Mornington Peninsula

The best Peninsula wine weekends improve dramatically when you stop treating accommodation as an afterthought. The right base means you drink once, eat properly, and wake up where the trip actually happens, instead of spending the whole weekend running transfers between Sorrento beds and Red Hill lunches.

Winery accommodation on the Mornington Peninsula is not a giant category. That is what makes it useful. The good options are limited, distinct, and easy to choose between once you know what sort of weekend you are building. Polperro Villas and Point Leo Estate Villas are for couples who want to stay close to the vineyard experience itself. Crittenden Estate Villas are stronger for a quieter bay-side wine-country crossover. Jackalope and Lindenderry sit slightly adjacent to the strict winery-villa category, but they belong in any serious wine-stay conversation because of how well they shape a Peninsula food-and-wine weekend.

How to use this category

Best for a one-night wine weekend?

Jackalope if you want drama and a stronger hotel experience. Polperro Villas if you want something quieter, more intimate, and directly embedded in the wine-country mood.

Best for a slower two-night stay?

Lindenderry, Point Leo Estate Villas, and Crittenden Estate Villas all work well when the weekend is less about ticking off cellar doors and more about pace, meals, and place.

Why this matters

The Peninsula's wine-country roads are beautiful but narrow and badly lit at night. The more you can collapse dining, tasting, and sleeping into the same zone, the better the weekend becomes.
