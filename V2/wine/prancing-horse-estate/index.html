Prancing Horse Estate

Organic and biodynamic estate that pours its own Pinot alongside imported Chablis and Burgundy — a genuine Old World–New World comparison tasting

Prancing Horse Estate · Red Hill

Prancing Horse is one of the few Peninsula cellar doors where the tasting deliberately includes wines made thousands of kilometres away. The estate is certified organic and biodynamic, the Pinot Noir and Chardonnay are thoughtful, but the real signature is a comparative flight that pours Prancing Horse's own bottles alongside carefully chosen Chablis and Burgundy from the owners' imported French portfolio. It is the most direct way to understand where Peninsula Chardonnay sits in the global conversation, and the most honest answer to the question of how close we are getting.

Tastings are intimate, by appointment, and often run by the owners themselves. The cellar door overlooks the estate vines with the bay visible through the trees; the Pinot Gris is an unexpected highlight if you arrive expecting only Pinot Noir.

This is the tasting you book when you have already worked through the obvious Peninsula estates and want a different kind of afternoon — one where the wine list keeps raising the stakes.
