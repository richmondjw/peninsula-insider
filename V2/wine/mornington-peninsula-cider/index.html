Mornington Peninsula Cider

Apple-forward ciders from a working orchard — a rustic tasting room and cheese boards in a real hinterland setting

Mornington Peninsula Cider · Main Ridge

Mornington Peninsula Cider operates out of a working apple orchard on the Main Ridge and is the right hinterland detour for anyone who has hit their limit on Pinot Noir for the day. The ciders are apple-forward, relatively dry by commercial standards, and the tasting room — a low, rustic shed surrounded by the orchard itself — doubles as a cheese and charcuterie stop for anyone ready to sit down for an hour.

Order the flight. Take the board. Walk into the orchard. The whole operation is small enough that a visit feels personal, and the cider pairs surprisingly well with the hinterland cheese you'll probably pick up on the same day.

A genuine change of pace stop on a cellar-door-heavy weekend.
