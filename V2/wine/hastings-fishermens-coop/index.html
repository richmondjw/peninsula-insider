Hastings Fishermen's Co-op

A working fishermen's co-op on the Western Port side — whatever came off the boats that morning, sold straight from the cabinet

Hastings Fishermen's Co-op · Hastings

Hastings Fishermen's Co-op is the bayside the Peninsula's tourist map forgets, and that is half the appeal. The co-op sits on the working side of Hastings pier, the cabinet is stocked with whatever the boats came in with that morning — whiting, flathead, calamari, Western Port mussels — and the prices are honest because the fish has travelled almost no distance from the water.

This is the supply side of several of the Peninsula's best seafood restaurants, so the quality is real. Buy for a holiday house dinner, take it back to whatever kitchen you have, and cook it simply. Whiting fried in butter with parsley and a lemon wedge is all the recipe you need.

A genuine Western Port stop that rewards anybody willing to cross the Peninsula for fresh fish.
