Red Hill Cheese

Small-batch cheesemaker with a tasting room — hard sheep's milk styles, washed rinds, and a rotating seasonal list

Red Hill Cheese · Red Hill

Red Hill Cheese is one of the Peninsula's quietest food landmarks. The tasting room is small and the range is deliberately tight: hard sheep's milk styles, a washed rind or two, a rotating seasonal list of whatever the cheesemaker is currently excited about. The pours are generous enough to understand what is happening on the plate, and the team is happy to talk through milk sourcing, ageing, and why a Peninsula block grows cheese the way it does.

Buy a wedge on the way out. Pair it with a loaf from Red Hill Bakery and a bottle from one of the estates down the road, and you have lunch sorted for a quiet afternoon at a holiday house.

Not a destination in its own right, but an essential stop on any serious Peninsula food weekend.
