Yabby Lake Vineyard

Tom Carson's single-block Chardonnay and Pinot Noir program, poured from a relaxed cellar door with one of the Peninsula's loveliest lunch decks

Yabby Lake Vineyard · Tuerong

Yabby Lake sits slightly east of the main wine-country road, and that off-axis position has worked in its favour. The Kirby family planted the vineyard in 1998 and hired Tom Carson to run the winemaking — and what the team has built in the years since is one of the most serious single-block programs in the country. The flagship Block 1 Pinot Noir became the first Pinot ever to win the Jimmy Watson Memorial Trophy in 2014. The Block 1 Chardonnay is, quietly, one of the best Chardonnays in Australia.

The cellar door is open daily and is deliberately more relaxed than the winemaking philosophy suggests. Seated tastings on the deck, a short early-lunch menu, and room to work through a full flight at your own pace. This is the cellar door we recommend when someone wants to understand why Peninsula Chardonnay is currently among the most interesting in the country.

Order the Block 1 flight. Stay for lunch. Buy the single-vineyard Chardonnay on the way out — it rewards five years in the cellar.
