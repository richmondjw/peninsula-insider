Small Stone Pantry

A wholefood-leaning pantry and café on the Point Nepean Road — grain bowls, good eggs, and a tiny retail shelf of Peninsula producers

Small Stone Pantry · Dromana

Small Stone Pantry is the one Dromana café that feels properly considered. The food leans wholefood without being preachy — grain bowls that actually taste of something, eggs done with a real herb garnish, house-made granola, pastries from a hinterland bakery — and the little retail shelf at the front doubles as a curated pantry of Peninsula producers: olive oils, honey, vinegars, salts, a few ferments.

It is the kind of café you stop at on the way down the bayside and leave with breakfast in your stomach and half a bag of things for the weekend's kitchen. The coffee is good, the service is quick, and the room is small enough that the morning feels personal.

Useful on a drive from Melbourne when you want to buy your weekend's supplies in one stop. Doubly useful as a mid-morning reset on a Red Hill day.
