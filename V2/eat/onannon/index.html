Onannon

Three winemakers, small-batch Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, and cult-favourite status among Australia's best sommeliers

Onannon · Tuerong

Onannon is the kind of Peninsula producer you find on the lists of the best restaurants in Melbourne and Sydney long before you find it at the supermarket. Three winemakers — Will Byron, Kaspar Hermann and Sam Middleton — formed the project years ago to make tiny batches of restrained, elegant Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, and the wines have become quiet cult favourites among the people who drink for a living.

The style is deliberate: low-intervention, minimal oak, acid-driven, built for the table rather than the trophy cabinet. The Main Ridge Pinot is the headline. The Foothills Chardonnay is the winemaker's pick.

Cellar door visits are limited and by appointment — this is a small project with day-jobs attached — so check the website for current availability. Go when you can. Buy a couple of bottles. You'll want them on a winter Sunday.
