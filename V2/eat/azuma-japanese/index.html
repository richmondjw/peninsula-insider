Azuma Japanese

A neighbourhood Japanese on Mornington's main street — reliable sushi, sashimi and donburi, and a useful break from the Peninsula's cheese-and-red-wine default

Azuma Japanese · Mornington

Azuma is a small, reliable neighbourhood Japanese on Mornington's main street and the right answer when a week of Peninsula cheese, charcuterie, and Pinot has pushed you toward something cleaner. The menu is classic middle-market Japanese — sushi and sashimi at the front, a tight list of donburi, tempura and ramen in the back — and the kitchen runs it all at an honest standard without trying to be inventive.

This is not a destination Japanese in the Melbourne sense. It is a solid neighbourhood option with a handful of seats at a small sushi counter, and a clear purpose in a town where the dining scene leans heavily toward pasta, pizza, and Peninsula wine restaurants. On a weeknight it is exactly what you want.

Book ahead for dinner. Weekday lunches are almost always walk-in friendly.
