Stonier Wines

One of the Peninsula's founding estates, planted in 1978 - grown-up, unflashy, with some of the oldest Chardonnay vines in the region

Stonier Wines · Merricks

Stonier was planted in 1978 by Brian and Noel Stonier and remains one of the Peninsula's quietly essential producers. Some of the oldest Chardonnay and Pinot Noir vines on the Peninsula are on this property, and the wines they make - particularly the Reserve Chardonnay and the KBS Vineyard Pinot Noir - are textbook cool-climate Peninsula: precise, saline, structured, built to age.

The cellar door is grown-up rather than flashy. There is no wedding marquee, no sculpture trail, no all-day pizza kitchen. There is a tasting counter, a winemaker's-list flight that walks you through block-level thinking, and a small seasonal food offering if you time the visit properly. This is the Peninsula cellar door to recommend to the visitor who has been unimpressed by the louder venues on the ridge.

Ask for the Reserve flight. Take home a bottle of the KBS Pinot - it is one of the Peninsula's more age-worthy wines, and you'll want to drink it in five years, not tonight.
