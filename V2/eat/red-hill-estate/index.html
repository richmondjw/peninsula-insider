Red Hill Estate

One of the Peninsula's most photographed cellar-door views, home to Max's Restaurant and a well-regarded traditional-method sparkling program

Red Hill Estate · Red Hill

Red Hill Estate has one of the most arresting cellar-door positions on the Peninsula — the slope falls away toward Western Port, French Island sits in the middle distance, and on clear days Phillip Island comes into view at the horizon. It is one of those rare Peninsula vineyards where the geography does most of the sales work before you even open a bottle.

Max's Restaurant has been pouring long lunches up here for decades and is still the right answer when visitors want the classic vineyard-dining-room Peninsula day. The traditional-method sparkling program is quietly one of the Peninsula's best: the Blanc de Blancs, in particular, rewards the drive, and the rosé is a year-round favourite on the estate's own lunch tables.

Book the window seats at Max's for lunch on a sunny autumn day. Arrive early, taste through the sparkling flight, walk the grounds while they prepare the table.
