Moorooduc Estate

Richard McIntyre's pioneering low-intervention estate — home to the Robinson Chardonnay, one of Australia's genuine Chardonnay reference points

Moorooduc Estate · Moorooduc

Moorooduc Estate is one of the quietest landmarks in Australian wine. Richard McIntyre has been making wine here for more than thirty years and is genuinely one of the country's elder statesmen of low-intervention, wild-yeast winemaking. The Robinson Chardonnay — made from a single block, wild-fermented, aged in old French oak — is a reference point for what Peninsula Chardonnay can be at the very top of its game.

The cellar door is open on weekends and for booked visits during the week. It is not a destination experience in the modern sense: no sculpture trail, no vineyard accommodation, no restaurant. It is a tasting room at a working winery where the winemaker himself is often in the room. Go when you want to understand what serious Chardonnay tastes like before anyone started chasing fashion.

Ask to taste the Robinson and the Garden Pinot back-to-back. Buy the Robinson. Decant it before you pour it.
