Rare Hare at Willow Creek

Jackalope's relaxed lunch room — wood fire, vineyard views, and plates built for sharing

Rare Hare at Willow Creek · Merricks

Rare Hare is the second restaurant at Jackalope and for many people it's the better of the two. Where Doot Doot Doot leans into fine-dining drama, Rare Hare takes the vineyard lunch brief and runs with it: wood-fired grill, long shared plates, a list of Willow Creek estate wines by the glass, and a room that opens onto the pinot rows.

The menu is structured around pizza, wood-fire mains, and a handful of raw starters that have become favourites — the cured kingfish, the burrata with stone fruit in season, whatever's running through the grill that afternoon. The bar is a genuinely good solo lunch spot. The sound system knows what it's doing.

Book a table on the terrace if you can, order one more bottle than you planned to, and let the afternoon stretch. This is daylight dining done honestly.
