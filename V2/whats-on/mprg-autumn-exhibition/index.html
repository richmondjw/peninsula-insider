Mornington Peninsula Regional Gallery — Autumn Exhibition

The Peninsula's most serious public art institution, running a rotating autumn exhibition calendar that's quietly one of the best regional programmes in Victoria.

Worth the drive Kids Grade B Rainy-day rescue

Editor\u2019s verdict

The Peninsula's best rainy-day cultural hour. Free, well curated, five minutes from Main Street. No excuse to skip it.

MPRG (Mornington Peninsula Regional Gallery) is the region's most serious public art institution and the Autumn 2026 exhibition programme covers contemporary Australian painting, a rotating photography wall, and a Peninsula-focused historical show in the side gallery. Entry is free.

The gallery sits on Dunns Road in Mornington, five minutes' drive from the Main Street restaurant strip. Allow 45 minutes to an hour, and pair with lunch in Mornington either side — the gallery fills the awkward post-lunch hour better than any cafe on the Peninsula.

For rainy-day weekends, MPRG + lunch on Main Street + an afternoon coffee is a complete three-hour loop that nobody needs to book.

Kids Grade B

Fine for school-age kids who already like galleries. Family programmes on select Sundays. Not a toddler move.

One age range event — good if you've got 5-to-10s, risky otherwise.
