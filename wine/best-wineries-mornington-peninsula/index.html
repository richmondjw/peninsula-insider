The Best Wineries on the Mornington Peninsula

The Mornington Peninsula is one of Australia's most consistently excellent cool-climate wine regions. With over 200 growers working the Red Hill plateau and its surrounding sub-regions, the question is no longer whether the drive is worth it — it's knowing which estates to visit and in what order. This list is editorial. No advertising, no paid placement. The wine has been drunk at the source.

The Peninsula's signature varieties are Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, shaped by a maritime climate and the free-draining basalt soils of the ridge. The best wineries here are not just producing strong wine — they're doing it in locations that justify sitting down for an hour. A tasting paired with a lunch at the right estate is still one of the best ways to spend a Peninsula day.

The list is organised by sub-region, with the Halliday-scored benchmark estates leading each section. Below them: the smaller rooms doing quiet, interesting work that rarely makes the tourism brochures.

Red Hill · Main Ridge · Red Hill South

The plateau — the Peninsula's wine heartland

The highest concentration of serious estates on the Peninsula. Kooyong, Paringa, Ocean Eight, and Dexter are all here — alongside a dense cluster of smaller producers that rewards a full day.

Planning your winery visit

What makes the Mornington Peninsula wine region different?

Scale and style. The Peninsula is a small, maritime-influenced region producing cool-climate wines at a fraction of the volume of the Yarra Valley or Barossa. The best Pinot Noirs here have a structural elegance — they're lighter in colour and weight than warmer-climate versions, with high acidity and fine tannin. The Chardonnays are restrained, minerally, and built for the table. This is food wine in a food region, which is why the combination of cellar door + restaurant works so well here.

Do I need to book cellar doors in advance?

Smaller boutique producers require appointments — often a week or more ahead in summer. Larger estates like Montalto, Crittenden, and Kooyong are generally open without bookings, though the restaurant tables always require a reservation. Check each estate's website before visiting; opening hours and booking requirements shift seasonally, and many reduce days in winter.

Red Hill vs Main Ridge vs Merricks

Red Hill and Red Hill South have the highest density of cellar doors in the smallest area — good for a self-drive half-day. Main Ridge is quieter, with the highest altitude estates (Ocean Eight, Dexter) producing the Peninsula's most structured wines. Merricks sits lower and warmer, closer to the coast — Polperro and Pt. Leo Estate define what this sub-region can do. A two-day visit can cover all three if you're not rushing.

What food goes with Mornington Peninsula wine?

The Peninsula's best restaurants have been designed around the local wine. Pinot Noir pairs with mushrooms, duck, salmon, and the wood-fired preparations that characterise places like Montalto, Paringa, and Ten Minutes by Tractor. Chardonnay works with anything briny — oysters, cured fish, the Peninsula's excellent cheese producers. The rule here is simpler than anywhere: drink what's made nearby with what's grown nearby.

Best Mornington Peninsula wineries for a day trip from Melbourne

Crittenden Estate in Dromana is the closest serious cellar door to Melbourne — about an hour down the freeway, with a strong tasting room and good grounds. From there, Red Hill South and Main Ridge are another 20 minutes. A day-trip circuit from Melbourne can realistically cover three or four estates if you leave early, pick estates in sequence, and resist turning lunch into a three-hour event (or embrace it).
