Garagiste

Barnaby Flanders' micro-production Chardonnay and Pinot project - rigour, restraint, and regular appearances in the Halliday Top 100

Book a tasting
Garagiste · Mornington

Garagiste is Barnaby Flanders' solo project and a study in small-batch restraint. Barnaby - who also co-founded Allies Wines - makes tiny quantities of Chardonnay and Pinot Noir under the Garagiste label, sourcing fruit from carefully chosen Peninsula blocks, and the bottles that result routinely punch into the Halliday Top 100 without any marketing theatre behind them.

The project is deliberately unscaled. There is no big cellar door, no restaurant, no gift shop. Visits are by appointment and usually take the form of a quiet conversation with Barnaby or a small team member in the working space where the wines are made. This is the kind of Peninsula producer who sells out through wine clubs and top restaurant lists before the bottles reach a shelf.

Check the website for current availability. If you can get on a tasting, go - this is the tight end of the Peninsula wine world and the wines deserve serious attention.
