Elgee Park

The Baillieu family's 1972 vineyard - the planting that started the modern Peninsula wine region

Book a tasting +61 3 5989 7338
Elgee Park · Merricks

Elgee Park is where the modern Mornington Peninsula wine industry actually began. Baillie Baillieu planted vines here in 1972, three years before Main Ridge Estate, at a time when Victoria's cool-climate wine region had yet to exist as a concept. The estate is still in family hands, the plantings still trace the original blocks, and the wines - Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and a quietly exceptional Viognier - carry the kind of restraint only decades of the same family farming the same soil can produce.

The cellar door is low-key, rural, and unhurried. There is no restaurant, no sculpture walk, no attempt to turn the visit into a day-trip set piece. You taste, you talk, and you leave understanding a little more about how the Peninsula wine region actually came to be. The Viognier is a surprise - barely anyone on the Peninsula still plants the variety, and Elgee Park has been making good ones for thirty years.

Open limited days - check ahead. Bring a genuine interest in Peninsula wine history; you will be rewarded.
