Kerri Greens

Tom McCarthy and Lucas Blanck's new-school Peninsula label - lighter-style Pinot, approachable Chardonnay, a cellar door that feels like a bar

Book a tasting
Kerri Greens · Merricks

Kerri Greens is the Peninsula's new-school face. Tom McCarthy and Lucas Blanck launched the label a few years ago with a clear intent to make lighter, more immediate wines than the regional default - Pinot Noir that doesn't need to be cellared before it can be drunk, Chardonnay that leans into freshness rather than heft - and built a cellar door to match. The experience is closer to a good wine bar than a country estate: small plates, natural pours, a garden, and a team that explains the wine without any of the Peninsula's traditional formality.

This is the cellar door to recommend to friends who find the ridge wineries a little too serious. The wines are genuine - Tom worked at several of the Peninsula's top estates before launching Kerri Greens - and the approach is a reminder that Peninsula wine does not have to be cloistered to be good.

Open weekends. Book ahead for bigger groups. The single-vineyard Pinot Noir is the bottle to go home with.
