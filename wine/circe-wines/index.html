Circe Wines

Dan Buckle's small-batch Pinot Noir project - individual block bottlings from Peninsula vineyards, on the wine list at the country's best restaurants

Book a tasting
Circe Wines · Mornington

Circe is one of those Peninsula producers you encounter on a restaurant list in Sydney or Melbourne long before you encounter it at a cellar door. Dan Buckle's project is built around small-batch Pinot Noir, with fruit sourced from several carefully chosen Peninsula vineyards and each block treated as its own bottling rather than blended down into one estate label. The wines are benchmark elegant - restrained, cool, structured for the table - and widely regarded among Australian sommeliers as among the region's most consistently interesting Pinots.

Cellar door access is limited and generally by appointment; the project does not maintain a full retail tasting room. For most visitors the best way to encounter Circe is on the wine list at one of the Peninsula's best dining rooms - Laura, Tedesca, Ten Minutes - where the team will pour a glass against something familiar and let the wine make its own case.

Ask your sommelier. Buy direct from the website when allocations are released. One of the Peninsula's quiet benchmarks.
