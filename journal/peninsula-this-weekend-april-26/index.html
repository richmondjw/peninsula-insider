Peninsula This Weekend — 26 to 27 April

The long weekend is almost over. The Peninsula is quieter now, and that is not a problem.

By The Peninsula Insider Facts verified 26 April 2026
Weekend PickerAutumnWhats OnMay Planning
Red Hill hinterland in late autumn

Peninsula This Weekend is our recurring Friday-useful dispatch: the plan we’d actually make, the backup we’d keep in our pocket, and the bit of weekend thinking we’d happily do for you.

The Peninsula after a long weekend has a particular quality. The car parks have cleared, the cafes are no longer running on adrenaline, and the villages are back to something closer to their actual selves. If you are still here on Sunday, that is the version of the Peninsula you get today.

Today’s best move

Do not try to extract a fourth day’s worth of activity from a three-day weekend.

The smarter Sunday is a foreshore walk, one lunch with enough margin around it to feel unhurried, and a deliberate absence of things on the itinerary. Sorrento’s back beach in late April has the kind of cold clarity that you cannot get in summer — the crowd has gone, the light is low, and the walk from the main beach carpark to Bridgewater Bay and back takes about an hour without effort.

Follow it with lunch at one of the Ocean Beach Road places that isn’t already closing for the afternoon. Keep the afternoon unscheduled.

This is what the Peninsula is actually for on a post-long-weekend Sunday. The visitors who try to convert it into a day-trip checklist will be on the freeway by 2pm wondering why it felt rushed.

The next real anchor — Red Hill Market, Saturday 2 May

The first Saturday of May is one of the better Red Hill Market days of the year.

The school holiday crowd has cleared. The produce stalls are still at full autumn strength — mushrooms, late-season greens, the last of the root vegetables before the hinterland goes properly cold. The hinterland air has the first genuine edge of winter in it, which makes the market feel earned in a way that summer editions rarely do.

Go before 10am. The stallholders are at their sharpest before the coach tours arrive, and the produce quality visibly drops as the morning progresses. Get there early, work through the stalls without a plan, then spend the afternoon south of the market in a cellar door or along the back roads. The move is to stay in the hinterland after the market closes, not to head back to Melbourne with a bag of vegetables and nothing else.

This is the weekend for readers who want the Peninsula to feel like a considered choice rather than a school-holiday default.

The evening worth booking

Allara Briggs Pattison at Peninsula Hot Springs — same day, Saturday 2 May.

This is a named artist, not a generic Sunday Sessions slot. Allara Briggs Pattison is a Yorta Yorta/Dja Dja Wurrung singer-songwriter with a genuine catalogue, and the amphitheatre pool at Peninsula Hot Springs is one of the better acoustic settings in Victoria when the conditions suit it. You are in thermal water, the stone stage is directly in front of you, and the late autumn light over the tea-tree does what it does.

It is included with standard bathing. Book the afternoon session. Arrive early to secure the stone steps near the stage — the amphitheatre pool fills fast when the artist is named rather than generic.

If you are already staying south for Red Hill Market, this is the clean evening anchor that closes the day properly.

What we would skip

The impulse to extend the long weekend into a fifth commitment.

The Peninsula works best when there is one serious anchor per day and enough space around it to feel like a place rather than a programme. Trying to stack Red Hill Market, two cellar doors, a beach walk, and a hot springs visit into the same Saturday will produce a day that feels busy and remembered as not much. Pick one or two things and do them at the right pace.

The same logic applies to the writers festival debrief lunch, the farewell coffee, and the three-stop drive home. Let the weekend finish cleanly.

The three best shapes for the next two weekends

If you want the short version, these are the combinations worth planning around:

  • Saturday 2 May: Red Hill Market early + Allara Briggs Pattison at PHS for the afternoon
  • Saturday 2 May: Red Hill Market + one cellar door + no evening obligation
  • Saturday 9 May: Kodomo no Hi at Peninsula Hot Springs for families — the best Peninsula family event in May

The Peninsula in early May is not trying to compete with autumn’s headline weekends. It does not need to. The quieter version is often the better one.
