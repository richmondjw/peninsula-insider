Peninsula Golf: Our Tier Guide to Every Course Worth Knowing About

Not all Peninsula golf is equally useful to readers. This guide separates the destination rounds from the club names, and the public-access standouts from the private mythology.

By The Peninsula Insider
GolfRankingsMornington PeninsulaGuidePublic Golf
Cape Schanck coastline — near the southern golf corridor of the Mornington Peninsula

Not every respected golf course on the Peninsula is equally useful to a reader. Some are destination rounds you can actually book. Some are excellent but private. Some are better as part of a stay-and-play weekend than as a standalone mission. And some are simply not worth pretending belong in the same conversation as the best.

This guide uses tiers rather than a flat ranking because access matters as much as quality. A course you cannot play is not useless — but it belongs in a different editorial category to one you can build a real weekend around.

Tier 1 — The destination-makers

St Andrews Beach Golf Course

The lead story. Tom Doak architecture, public access, and genuine global ranking credibility. This is the course that changes the Peninsula’s entire editorial case for golf. You can book it tomorrow. It does not require a member.

Worth building a trip around: yes. Worth prioritising over everything else if you only have one round: yes.

Go autumn. Book early for weekends.

The National Golf Club

Arguably the strongest overall golf property on the Peninsula, with three courses and internationally significant design. The catch is real: this is a private members club. Most readers cannot simply book a tee time. It matters as context, as aspiration, and as the answer to “what is the absolute best golf on the Peninsula.” It is not a practical day-trip recommendation for most visitors.

Tier 2 — The serious public-access weekender set

Moonah Links

A proper anchor for a two-night golf weekend. The Open Course was built specifically for Australian Open use and plays accordingly — demanding, exposed, and worth respecting. The resort layer (accommodation, food, proximity to the hot springs) is what makes it function as a whole weekend rather than just a round.

The Dunes

One of the Peninsula’s most consistently respected public-access courses and still the most defensible Tier 2 recommendation for a visitor who wants a strong round without the resort scaffolding. The Cups layout is the other option on site — shorter, different, useful on day two.

Rosebud Country Club

Two courses, public access, well-regarded regionally. Less architectural mythology than the top pair but genuinely practical. Good value relative to the Tier 1 experience.

Tier 3 — Strong rounds with narrower fit

Mornington Golf Club — solid, accessible, useful if you are based in Mornington.

RACV Cape Schanck — the resort course at the southern tip. Scenic, convenient if you are staying there, but the golfing case is weaker than the location case.

Portsea Golf Club — semi-private access rules apply and change seasonally. The course is regarded well. Call ahead before counting on a tee time.

Flinders Golf Club — honest public-access course, quieter, worth a look if you are already in the area.

Sorrento Golf Club — access requires direct confirmation. Do not assume visitor availability without checking.

Tier 4 — Local and community play

The Peninsula has a broader landscape of community and municipal courses that serve locals well but are not destination rounds for visitors travelling specifically for golf. They are not worth listing here in detail — not because they are bad, but because they belong in a different category.

How to use this guide

One serious round, first visit: St Andrews Beach, no hesitation.

Golf weekend, two rounds: St Andrews Beach plus Moonah Links or The Dunes.

Travelling with mixed group: Prioritise the hotel layer — Peppers at Moonah Links, the resort at RACV Cape Schanck — so the non-golfers have something good to do while the round happens.

Chasing private prestige: The National is the answer, but work out the access reality before you build a trip around it.

Before you book

Green fees across all Peninsula courses are variable and should be confirmed directly at booking time. Access rules, particularly for semi-private clubs, change seasonally. This guide is an editorial frame, not a live pricing or availability tool.

The honest version of Peninsula golf is this: the public-access tier is better than most of Australia gives it credit for. Start there, not with the private mythology.
