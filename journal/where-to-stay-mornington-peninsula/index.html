Where to Stay on the Mornington Peninsula

Australia's first MICHELIN Two Keys hotel, a former Victorian institution turned IHG five-star, and a geothermal glamping village in Fingal. Here's what's actually on the Peninsula, how to choose between them, and how to tell two famous Sorrento properties apart.

By The Peninsula Insider Facts verified 22 April 2026
AccommodationLuxuryGlampingWinery StayBoutique HotelsHot SpringsFamily
Jackalope Hotel at Merricks North with vineyard views

In 2025, Jackalope Hotel became one of the first Australian properties to receive the MICHELIN Two Keys designation. It’s a useful marker for where Peninsula accommodation has arrived: 45 rooms in a design-forward winery hotel, two hatted restaurants, and an international hospitality identity that extends well beyond the regional weekend market.

That doesn’t mean the Peninsula is all Jackalope. There are 20+ accommodation options across three tiers — from five-star heritage hotels and $624-a-night thermal springs villas to canvas glamping tents minutes from geothermal pools. The question is matching the right property to the right trip.

One important note before we go further.

Two Sorrento properties that are not the same

Hotel Sorrento (5–15 Hotham Road) and InterContinental Sorrento (23 Constitution Hill Road) are completely separate properties. They share a postcode, a general location, and a century-plus of Peninsula history. They do not share ownership, buildings, management, or brand positioning.

Hotel Sorrento is a boutique pub hotel at 5–15 Hotham Road. 150+ year history; five-year renovation completed January 2026. The limestone basement now houses Shi Hui Shi, a Cantonese restaurant from chef Junlin Yi (ex-Red Spice Road). Outdoor pool. Approximately $309/night. Website: hotelsorrento.com.au.

InterContinental Sorrento — also known as “The Continental” — is the IHG five-star at 23 Constitution Hill Road. It opened August 2022 after a $100M+ redevelopment of the original 1875 Continental Hotel. Clifftop position, heated outdoor bay-view pool with cabanas, Aurora Spa & Bathhouse, multiple dining venues. Minimum check-in age 18. From approximately $595/night at launch. Website: thecontinentalsorrento.com.au.

Never conflate these properties in print or recommendation. Different buildings. Different streets. Different expectations.

Luxury tier

Jackalope Hotel — Merricks North

The MICHELIN Two Keys property. 45 rooms on the Willow Creek Vineyard estate; no children under 12. Vineyard infinity pool, heated spa, infrared sauna. Lair and Vineyard Bath rooms include deep-soak Japanese baths. Two restaurants: Doot Doot Doot (one hat GFG 2025, dinner nightly from 6pm) and Rare Hare (one hat GFG 2025, walk-in from 2pm weekends, wood-fired outdoor dining). Estimated $400–600+/night — verify current rates. Minimum 2-night stay on weekends likely.

166 Balnarring Road, Merricks North. jackalopehotels.com

InterContinental Sorrento — Sorrento (23 Constitution Hill Road)

IHG five-star at the clifftop above Sorrento. Opened August 2022 on the site of the 1875 Continental Hotel — $100M+ redevelopment. Heated outdoor pool with cabanas; views across Port Phillip Bay. Aurora Spa & Bathhouse with thermal bathing pools and full treatment menu. CeeCee’s all-day dining plus Pool Deck Bar. Minimum check-in age 18.

Note: SEPARATE from Hotel Sorrento at 5–15 Hotham Road — see disambiguation above.

23 Constitution Hill Road, Sorrento. thecontinentalsorrento.com.au

RACV Cape Schanck Resort — Cape Schanck

The Peninsula’s largest resort, 184 rooms. Five-star RACV property adjacent to Cape Schanck lighthouse. Championship golf course on-site. One Spa (massage, facials, hammam). Four dining venues including Cape, Samphire, and family-friendly Mantellina. RACV member benefits apply. The scale makes it the best option for families and groups who want full resort facilities.

racv.com.au

Lancemore Lindenderry Red Hill — Red Hill

40 rooms in a country house hotel. The Dining Room holds two hats in the AGFG 2025/2026 — dinner Thursday to Saturday, lunch Friday to Sunday. Lake views, tall silver birch trees, formal gardens. Recognised by Luxury Travel Magazine as one of Australia’s most romantic regional hotels. From approximately $300–600+/night.

142 Arthurs Seat Road, Red Hill. lancemore.com.au

Hotel Sorrento — Sorrento (5–15 Hotham Road)

Boutique pub hotel, 150+ year history, five-year renovation completed January 2026. Outdoor pool. Shi Hui Shi Cantonese restaurant in the limestone basement. From approximately $309/night. 4-star.

Note: SEPARATE from InterContinental Sorrento at 23 Constitution Hill Road.

hotelsorrento.com.au

Alba Sanctuary — Fingal

The Sanctuary accommodation at Alba Thermal Springs: 5 hilltop villas plus 2 premium rooms, all adults-only (minimum age 18). Access to Alba’s geothermal pool complex on-site. Slippers, robes, and complimentary minibar included. From $624/night (verify current pricing). 5-minute drive from Peninsula Hot Springs.

282 Browns Road, Fingal. albathermalsprings.com.au

Winery accommodation

Polperro Villas (Red Hill)

Four villas plus a farmhouse on the Polperro estate. In-room spa bath in all villas; Villa 4 has a private sauna. Continental breakfast in villa on arrival, open fireplace, private deck. 2-night minimum Friday/Saturday; 3-night minimum public holidays. From approximately $252–327/night standard. Hatted restaurant on-site.

Port Phillip Estate (Red Hill South)

Six luxury suites on the winery estate, adjacent to the hatted restaurant with vineyard-to-bay panoramic views. Freshly squeezed OJ breakfast. Small-scale; design quality.

Crittenden Lakeside Villas (Dromana)

Three self-contained villas built over the vineyard lake. Spa bath, fully equipped kitchen, lake and vineyard views. From approximately $300–450/night. Restaurant on-site trading under a new name post-renovation — verify crittendenwines.com.au before booking dining.

Flinders Hotel Quarters (Flinders)

40 architecturally designed rooms in Flinders village, adjacent to the Flinders Hotel pub. Curated MP wine minibar. Walking distance to art galleries and coastal walks. Near Foxeys Hangout and Pt. Leo Estate. From approximately $250–350/night.

Glamping

Peninsula Hot Springs Glamping & Eco Lodges (Fingal)

On the grounds of Peninsula Hot Springs, 140 Springs Lane. 10 glamping tents in three styles plus 3 premium eco lodges. All have geothermal floor heating, king beds, private decks, en suite, split system. Full access to the geothermal pool complex. Healthy buffet breakfast included. Pricing not published — check the website directly.

Iluka Retreat & Camp (Shoreham)

15-hectare property near Red Hill South wineries and Ashcombe Maze. Canvas bell tents with queen beds. Camp kitchen, BBQ, obstacle challenge course. Family-friendly.

Yurt Hideaway (Tootgarook)

Single yurt (exclusive property booking). 800m walk to Capel Sound beach. 1 hour from Melbourne. Self-catering.

By trip purpose

Couples (luxury): Jackalope Hotel (MICHELIN Two Keys, min age 12), InterContinental Sorrento (min age 18), Lancemore Lindenderry, Alba Sanctuary (adults-only 18+).

Families: RACV Cape Schanck Resort (largest, most facilities), Portsea Village Resort (1–3 bedroom apartments, indoor pool, tennis court), Plantation House at Whitecliffs (family rooms, 300m from beach).

Golf: RACV Cape Schanck Resort, Peppers Moonah Links Resort (home of the 2005 Australian Open; complimentary shuttle to Peninsula Hot Springs).

Spa and wellness: InterContinental Sorrento (Aurora Spa & Bathhouse), RACV Cape Schanck (One Spa), Peninsula Hot Springs Glamping (on-site pool access), Alba Sanctuary (geothermal on-site).

Wine region base: Polperro Villas, Port Phillip Estate, Crittenden Lakeside Villas, Flinders Hotel Quarters.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best luxury hotel? Jackalope Hotel holds the MICHELIN Two Keys designation and is the most internationally recognised. InterContinental Sorrento is the most full-service five-star. Different characters: Jackalope is winery-design-food; the InterContinental is heritage-coastal-spa.

Hotel Sorrento vs InterContinental — what’s the difference? Different buildings, different streets. Hotel Sorrento: 5–15 Hotham Rd, boutique pub hotel, Jan 2026 renovation complete. InterContinental: 23 Constitution Hill Rd, IHG five-star, opened Aug 2022 on the 1875 Continental site.

Best stay near Peninsula Hot Springs? The glamping tents at Peninsula Hot Springs itself (on-site, 140 Springs Lane). Alba Sanctuary 5 minutes away (18+ adults-only, from $624/night). Peppers Moonah Links runs a free shuttle to the springs.

Family-friendly options? RACV Cape Schanck Resort (most complete), Portsea Village Resort (self-catering apartments), Plantation House at Whitecliffs (300m from beach). Jackalope (no under-12s) and Alba Sanctuary (18+) are adults-only.
