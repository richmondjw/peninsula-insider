Welcome to a small inbox.

You've just signed up to Peninsula This Weekend, which means once a week — Thursday morning, 7am — a single email will land here from me. No team, no rotation of guest writers, no "this week's curated picks from our partners." Just one editor who actually lives on the Peninsula, has opinions about it, and is writing for the kind of person who'd rather know one good place to book than scroll fifteen.

The first proper email lands next Thursday. Before then, here's the shape of it.

Every Thursday

What lands every Thursday.

01

The editor's line

Three or four sentences from me about what's on my mind this week. Dated. Weather-aware. Sometimes grumpy.

02

This weekend

Three picks — Friday dinner, Saturday lunch, Sunday morning. Bookable. Specific. Tested.

Worth knowing

Small notes. A new opening, a chef who's moved, a road that's closed for the long weekend. The kind of thing you'd want a local friend to mention.

04

The cellar door

One winery, one reason to go this month. Often the quiet ones.

The signature block. An honest call on what to skip, and why. The part you'll either love or unsubscribe over.

06

From the journal

A longer piece, if you want to keep reading after the letter ends.

While you're waiting.

If you happen to be on the Peninsula this weekend — or you're trying to decide whether to be — here's one we'd put in next Thursday's email if it weren't already going to you now.

Walk Bushrangers Bay before 10am.

Cape Schanck car park, ninety minutes round-trip, mostly downhill on the way out and a quiet climb back. The cliffs do something to the light that the photos never quite get. Take a coffee from the kiosk on the way in, don't try to push past the bay — it's the destination, not a staging post — and you'll be back at your car before the day-trippers have found the gate.

One last thing.

This email comes from a real inbox. If you've got a weekend coming up and you're stuck between two places, or you've just had something brilliant somewhere I should know about, just hit reply. I read everything. I usually answer within a day.

See you Thursday at 7.

— James

Editor, Peninsula This Weekend. Writing from a desk in Sorrento.
