Trofeo Estate

The Peninsula's amphora winery — terracotta-aged wines, an Italian-leaning range, and a cellar that is worth the visit on its own

Trofeo Estate · Dromana

Trofeo Estate is the Peninsula's one and only amphora winery. The team ages a meaningful portion of the estate's production in terracotta pots rather than oak barrels, and the resulting wines — Sangiovese, Fiano, Nebbiolo — have a textural depth and a savoury edge that nothing else in the region produces. The ancient method sits oddly against the Peninsula's cool-climate reputation and that is exactly why it works.

The barrel room itself is worth the visit. Stepping into a Peninsula cellar and finding it lined with huge clay amphorae rather than the expected French oak is the kind of small shock that reorients how you think about the region's possibilities. The tasting is generous and the team is happy to explain the winemaking in genuine detail.

Book the cellar tour. Taste the Sangiovese and the Fiano back-to-back. Bring home whichever one most surprises you.
