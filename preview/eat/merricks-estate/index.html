Merricks Estate

A small, long-standing estate with one of the Peninsula's rare serious Shiraz programs and a windswept coastal vineyard

Merricks Estate · Merricks

Merricks Estate is one of those Peninsula cellar doors that has resisted all temptation to grow. George and Jacky Kefford planted the estate in 1977 on a windswept block close to Western Port — one of the coolest, most exposed positions in the region — and the estate is still small, still family-run, and still committed to the same short line-up of wines: Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and, unusually for the Peninsula, a serious Shiraz.

The Shiraz is the conversation piece. Only a handful of Peninsula producers take the variety seriously; Merricks Estate has done so for four decades and the bottlings are distinctive — peppery, cool-climate, closer in spirit to the northern Rhône than to the Barossa. The tasting room is quiet and unvarnished, the pour is generous, and the whole visit feels like stepping slightly off the main cellar-door circuit.

Go on a weekday for the quietest experience. Buy a bottle of the Shiraz. Drink it with winter lamb.
