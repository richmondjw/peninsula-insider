Peninsula This Weekend - 24 to 26 April

ANZAC dawn is the weekend's clearest call. Around it, the Peninsula is offering a reflective long-weekend mix of writers, walks, warm water, and a few better decisions than the obvious crowd-chase.

If you only do two things this weekend

The clearest event anchors shaping the Peninsula from 24 to 26 April.

3 picks
Every Sunday Live Music

Peninsula Hot Springs - Sunday Sessions

Rye

Live acoustic music every Sunday afternoon from the amphitheatre pool. Included with bathing. The category's most under-published recurring live music product.

Editor's verdict

A strong soft-Sunday play if you're already staying nearby. Worth keeping on the list, but only if the live artist lineup still looks good when you book.

18 April – 30 April 2026 Wellness

Alba Thermal Springs - Fire & Ice Circuits

Red Hill

The quieter Peninsula thermal complex is running a limited April Fire & Ice circuit series, with silent sessions and sunrise slots that make the category make sense again.

Editor's verdict

The sharpest late-afternoon thermal session on the Peninsula right now. Worth booking if you want a rain-proof adult reset that still feels like an outing.

23 April – 26 April 2026 Writers & Ideas

Sorrento Writers Festival 2026

Sorrento

Four days of Australia's sharpest writers in a weatherboard village at the tip of the Peninsula - the one festival worth planning a whole weekend around.

Editor's verdict

Book the Sorrento weekend, not the day trip. The festival is pitched for people who want to eat, walk and read all three days - not tick off two sessions and drive home tired.

Main Ridge

The Peninsula's most reliably kid-proof outing. Rows of strawberries, a tractor ride, a box to fill, and a cafe that deals in volume at the other end.

First-timers to the Peninsula travelling with kids: this is the clean first move. Pick, pay, picnic, walk.

25 April 2026 Civic

ANZAC Day Dawn Service - Sorrento Memorial

Sorrento

The Peninsula's most quietly moving civic event. A dawn service at the Sorrento memorial, a community breakfast at the RSL, and a village that drops its tourist costume for a morning.

Editor's verdict

If you're already staying in Sorrento for Writers Festival weekend, the Saturday morning dawn service is one of the most quietly moving hours on the Peninsula calendar. Not worth a special drive from Melbourne - worth going to if you're already there.

Red Hill

The region's winter cellar-door weekend - fires lit, whites cellared, and the one time of year you can walk into the top 20 wineries without an appointment.
