Sunny Ridge Strawberry Farm - School Holiday Picking

The Peninsula's most reliably kid-proof outing. Rows of strawberries, a tractor ride, a box to fill, and a cafe that deals in volume at the other end.

Worth the drive First timer Kids Grade A Sunny only

Editor's verdict

First-timers to the Peninsula travelling with kids: this is the clean first move. Pick, pay, picnic, walk.

Sunny Ridge is the Peninsula's baseline family activity and the cleanest win if you're travelling with kids and the ridge cellar doors are off-limits for the day. The strawberry season runs from late spring into the autumn shoulder - and the April school holidays are typically the last reliable picking window before the vines wind down.

The move: arrive 9:30am before the coach tours, pay entry, get issued a box, walk the rows for 20 minutes, pay by weight. The cafe at the end turns strawberries into everything from milkshakes to jam scones, and the outdoor tables are wide enough for a family of six without queuing.

Rainy days: picking closes. Check the Instagram the morning of.

Kids Grade A

Bring a two-year-old, bring a twelve-year-old, bring all of them. The Peninsula's single most forgiving family activity when one kid is tired and another is not.

Bring a pram, bring a toddler, bring a teen - everyone will be happy.
