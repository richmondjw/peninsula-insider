Alba Thermal Springs - Fire & Ice Circuits

The quieter Peninsula thermal complex runs its hot-and-cold bathing circuit year-round, with dedicated silent sessions and sunrise slots that make the category make sense again.

Worth the drive First timer Adults only All weather

Editor's verdict

The category has been broken by Peninsula Hot Springs' theme-park scale for a decade. Alba fixes it. Book the morning session, do the full circuit, eat on the ridge after.

Alba's ongoing thermal bathing experience is the strongest year-round wellness case the Peninsula makes. The ticketing caps bather numbers, the architecture earns the price, and the hot-and-cold circuit is properly sequenced rather than dumped into a single muddy lawn. For first-timers who've heard the 'Peninsula thermal springs' category described and been confused by which version is worth the drive, this is the answer.

Book the 10:00am session. Do the full hot circuit twice, the cold plunges three times, the steam once, and stay for a late breakfast at the on-site cafe before driving up Mornington-Flinders Road for an afternoon walk or cellar door stop. The complete day, from car park to car park, is about four hours.

Silent sessions and sunrise bathing run on a rotating weekly schedule - check the calendar on the night of booking. Both are worth setting an alarm for.

Adults only

Alba is the Peninsula's adults-only thermal option. Leave the kids with the grandparents; this is the point.

Leave them at home. This is not the outing.
