Peninsula Hot Springs - Sunday Sessions

Live acoustic music every Sunday afternoon from the amphitheatre pool. Included with bathing. The category's most under-published recurring live music product.

Worth the drive First timer Kids Grade B All weather

Editor's verdict

The Peninsula's only weekly live music product that no other publication lists. Book a lazy Sunday afternoon, eat a late lunch on the ridge first, arrive at 2pm for the amphitheatre pool.

Peninsula Hot Springs runs a rolling Sunday Sessions acoustic programme from the amphitheatre pool most weeks of the year - and it's a product so under-published outside the Hot Springs' own marketing that even locals don't know it happens. It is genuinely charming: you're in thermal water, a folk or acoustic duo is playing on the stone stage, and the afternoon light over the tea-tree makes the whole thing feel like a film still.

It is included with bathing, which means the cost of entry is whatever your standard day pass costs. Book the 2:00pm slot to catch the 3:00pm set; check the Hot Springs 'What's On' page the week of for the artist. The amphitheatre pool fills up fast - arrive 45 minutes early to secure the stone steps.

Not to be confused with the stand-alone ticketed music events they run a few times a year.

Kids Grade B

Kids love the bathing, but the Sunday Sessions crowd is older and the music programming is for adults. Good for families with confident swimmers aged 8+.

One age range event - good if you've got 5-to-10s, risky otherwise.
