Dining

Where to Eat

The peninsula's dining scene has undergone a quiet revolution over the past decade. The combination of extraordinary local produce — seafood, dairy, lamb, vegetables from rich volcanic soils — and a highly competitive wine country environment has pushed restaurant quality well above what you'd expect for a regional area.

Jackalope Hotel
Doot Doot Doot
Merricks North
★★★★★

Jackalope's headline fine diner is theatrical, indulgent, and genuinely excellent. Chef delivers a menu built around exceptional local produce with a commitment to seasonality and wine pairing. Caviar features. Long degustation format. One of the peninsula's true bucket-list meals.

Degustation · Book Months Ahead
🍽️
Fine Dining
Laura at Point Leo Estate
Merricks
★★★★★

Chef Josep Espuga's European-leaning fine diner sits within the remarkable Point Leo Estate — a working winery and 16-acre sculpture park. Among the very finest tables on the peninsula. The view from the terrace as you finish lunch is simply spectacular.

Fine Dining · Sculpture Park
🍽️
Hatted
Ten Minutes by Tractor
Main Ridge
★★★★★

Elegant, produce-driven dishes matched with the winery's award-winning cellar. The dining combines silver service with genuine country warmth — it never feels stuffy. The views over vineyards and bushland are as good as any in the region.

Hatted · Silver Service
🍽️
Italian · Farm-to-Table
Tedesca Osteria
Red Hill
★★★★★

Chef Brigitte Hafner and wine director James Broadway's intimate 30-seater feels like the most brilliant dinner party you've ever attended. Rustic, honest Italian-inflected cooking from exceptional local produce. One of the most purely enjoyable meals on the peninsula.

Italian · 30 seats only
🍽️
Winery Restaurant
Paringa Estate Restaurant
Red Hill South
★★★★★

Set on a winery with some of the oldest vines on the peninsula, Paringa's restaurant has grown its own considerable reputation. Polished, confident cooking with outstanding matched wines from their estate. The outdoor terrace in autumn is divine.

Winery Restaurant · Hatted
🍽️
Views · Estate
Port Phillip Estate
Red Hill South
★★★★★

Some of the finest food, wine and views on the entire peninsula. The restaurant commands panoramic views over the estate and Port Phillip Bay beyond — the visual payoff alone is worth the visit. The cooking is confident and local-produce focused.

Views · Estate Dining
Insider

For a special occasion meal at the right price point: Laura at Point Leo combines extraordinary quality with a slightly more accessible format than Doot Doot Doot — and the sculpture park walk before or after makes it a genuinely complete experience rather than just dinner.

🌿 Casual & Relaxed
🔥
Share Plates
Rare Hare
Merricks North (Jackalope)

Long communal tables, woodfired cooking, share plates and Willow Creek wines by the glass. The vibe is convivial, the food is genuinely excellent. Saturday lunch here is one of the great peninsula pleasures.

Woodfired · Share Plates
🌿
Gardens · Winery
Montalto
Red Hill South

Seven vineyards, more than 30 acres of vines, and a restaurant that delivers confident, produce-driven cooking alongside excellent estate wines. The gardens and sculpture trail are an added bonus — wander before or after lunch.

Gardens · Sculpture Trail
🍷
Views · Wine
Polperro Winery
Red Hill

Panoramic views of vines and native trees, clean modern interiors, and a share-plate menu that draws from all directions with genuine skill. One of the peninsula's quintessential long-lunch venues.

Share Plates · Views
🌊
Mediterranean
Martha's Table
Safety Beach

Marine-hued dining room, casual aperitivo bar and in-house provedore — this handsome all-in-one brings Mediterranean living to the marina. One of the more beautiful rooms on the peninsula.

Marina Setting
🍖
American BBQ
Red Gum BBQ
Red Hill

Rolled up sleeves, airy warehouse, picnic tables and excellent American BBQ: pulled pork, beef brisket, smoked chicken. A rollicking antidote to the fancy winery restaurant circuit.

BBQ · Casual · Hearty
🥐
French Bistro
Bistro Elba
Sorrento

Beach-y, artfully dilapidated French bistro with twice-baked cheese soufflé, gin-cured ocean trout and a wine list of small emerging French producers. The kind of place that feels like a lucky find every time.

French · Sorrento
☕ Breakfast & Brunch
🌅
Brunch · Winery
Foxey's Hangout
Red Hill

Laidback, sunny, spacious — set on a lovely estate with an open kitchen and outdoor deck. Easy-drinking wines and a generous brunch menu. One of the most relaxed morning spots in the hinterland.

Outdoors · Relaxed
🥞
French · Crepes
Ouest France Bistro
Mornington

Once a creperie, now a charming French bistro bringing Brittany to the peninsula. Classic dishes, Breton specialties and excellent coffee in a small, intimate setting. Look for the blue-and-white awning.

French · Breton
🍾
Cellar Door
Yabby Lake Cellar Door
Tuerong

Opens for tastings from mid-morning and the relaxed restaurant does excellent two- or three-course lunches. Arrive early, taste the single-vineyard chardonnay before lunch — one of the peninsula's most consistent wine experiences.

Early Lunch · Single Vineyard
🍝
Italian
Mr Vincenzo's
Mornington

1970s Italian vibes, large-format pastas, and a genuinely fun atmosphere. Ideal for a laid-back late morning or early lunch when you want something hearty and characterful.

Italian · Character
April

April is truffle season in Victoria — several peninsula restaurants and producers incorporate fresh local truffles into their menus from late April. Ask your restaurant about seasonal truffle features when booking.
