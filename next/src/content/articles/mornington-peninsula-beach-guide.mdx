Most visitors arrive on the Mornington Peninsula thinking of "the beach" as one thing. It isn't.

The peninsula has three coastlines, and each one plays by different rules. On the west, Port Phillip Bay laps at calm, shallow "front" beaches that sit beside the towns — Sorrento, Mornington, Mount Martha, Rye, Rosebud. On the south, Bass Strait hammers the "back" beaches — Gunnamatta, Sorrento Back Beach, Portsea Back, Flinders Ocean — a rugged surf coast inside the Mornington Peninsula National Park. On the east, Western Port Bay is quieter again: tidal, low-key, with long stretches of sand at Balnarring, Somers, and Flinders' north end.

Which coastline you want depends on who's with you. A toddler and a paddleboard? Bay. A surfer or a photographer chasing cliffs? Ocean. A dog that needs to run? Almost certainly Western Port or one of the Shire's off-leash zones — because dogs are not permitted anywhere inside Mornington Peninsula National Park.

This is the full guide: where to swim, where to surf, where to walk, what's patrolled, and what's currently closed.

---

## Three coastlines, one peninsula

The shape of the peninsula is the whole story. It's a curving spit of land pointing north-west from Melbourne, separating Port Phillip Bay on one side from the open ocean on the other, with Western Port lapping at its eastern flank.

### Port Phillip Bay: the "front" beaches

These are the beaches most first-time visitors picture: calm, shallow, sandy, with colourful bathing boxes and a pier nearby. Mothers Beach in Mornington is generally flat and calm — the pier's positioning shields it from chop. Further south, Sorrento Front, Rye Front, Rosebud and Safety Beach offer the same protected water: good for young children, paddleboarding, and anyone who doesn't want to deal with surf.

Front beaches are where the Shire's seasonal **beach matting** appears each November, running until around late April or May at Mt Martha, Mornington's Mothers Beach, Rosebud (near the jetty), Rye Front (near the pier) and Sorrento Front (near The Baths). Mount Martha also keeps two floating **MobiChairs** available on weekends during patrol hours — they float, so wheelchairs can enter the water. Call the Mount Martha Life Saving Club on (03) 5974 4140 to confirm current patrol dates.

### Bass Strait: the "back" or ocean beaches

Drive fifteen minutes across the peninsula from Sorrento town and you're in a different country. The ocean beaches sit inside the Mornington Peninsula National Park, with powerful surf, weathered cliffs, and real rip currents. Sorrento Back Beach, Portsea Back, Gunnamatta, Rye Ocean, St Andrews, Koonya, Flinders Ocean — all ocean, all rugged.

Three are **patrolled** — Sorrento Back, Portsea Back and Gunnamatta — on summer weekends and during school holidays only. The rest are unpatrolled; Parks Victoria explicitly advises against swimming at Rye Ocean. Gunnamatta is the most popular surf beach in the National Park and, per Visit Mornington Peninsula, the most hazardous for swimming. Swim between the flags.

**Dogs are not permitted anywhere in the National Park** — not on the beaches, not in the car parks, not in Greens Bush. The management plan was amended in October 2016 and has not changed since.

### Western Port: the quieter east

Western Port is where locals often go when the rest of the peninsula is busy. Balnarring, Somers, Point Leo, Shoreham, Flinders' north end — these beaches are tidal, lower-key, and friendlier to dogs. Flinders Beach's north end, between Dodds Creek and Manton Creek, is off-leash year-round. Tassells Cove at Safety Beach and Hawker Beach at Mount Martha are also year-round off-leash.

Ocean beaches not in the table above — Rye Ocean, St Andrews, Koonya, Flinders Ocean, Bushrangers Bay — can still be visited, but swimming is not advised at several of them.

Patrol seasons vary year to year. Check Life Saving Victoria's beach safety feed before any weekend visit.

## Where you can (and can't) bring your dog

### Mornington Peninsula National Park — no dogs, ever

Inside the National Park — which includes every ocean back beach plus Greens Bush — dogs are prohibited. On beaches. In car parks. On the Two Bays Walk. There is no seasonal window, no before-9am loophole. This rule was locked in by a management plan amendment in October 2016.

<AlertBlock type="danger" heading="Active alert — PAPP fox baiting">
Fox control baiting with PAPP poison is active from Portsea Ocean Beach through to Greens Bush until October 2026. PAPP is lethal to dogs. If your dog is suspected of having ingested a bait, go to a vet immediately — the antidote is methylene blue.
</AlertBlock>

### Year-round off-leash beaches

Six confirmed year-round off-leash sections: Tassells Cove (Safety Beach), Hawker Beach (Mount Martha), Somers Beach (east of the yacht club — with some time-of-day variation), Fossil Beach (Mornington), Flinders Beach north end (between Dodds Creek and Manton Creek), and Anthony's Nose (Dromana).

### The standard Shire rule

For most other Shire-managed bay beaches, dogs are permitted on-leash. During daylight saving, they're prohibited from the sand between 9am and 7pm. Some beaches apply stricter local zones. Check the Mornington Peninsula Shire's current beach controls before you go — rules change, and Shire signage outranks any travel post.

## Beaches by town

**Sorrento.** Two completely different beach days, five minutes apart. The Front Beach (bay side) is calm and accessible — a 40m beach ramp near the kiosk is the best wheelchair access on the ocean side of the peninsula. The Back Beach (ocean side) is surfed and photographed; rock pools large enough to swim in at low tide.

**Portsea.** The Front Beach is a scuba-diving hub with historic jetties and boat sheds. The Back Beach runs along weathered Bass Strait cliffs and is patrolled in summer.

**Mount Martha.** A two-kilometre stretch divided by Balcombe Creek. The southern end is patrolled and home to the most accessible beach setup on the bay: daily beach matting, floating MobiChairs on weekends.

**Rye and Rosebud.** Shallow, family-friendly bay beaches. Rye Pier is one of the best snorkelling spots on the peninsula — warm, about 6m deep, regularly occupied by divers. Rosebud has extensive foreshore parklands and BBQs.

**Safety Beach.** Tassells Cove is year-round off-leash and a favourite with locals for exactly that reason.

**Flinders.** Two entirely different beaches sharing one name. Flinders Ocean Beach (Bass Strait side) is a snorkelling and surf spot — currently affected by a landslip west of Mushroom Reef. Flinders Beach (Western Port side) is a 3km stretch with off-leash dog access at the north end.

**Balnarring and Point Leo.** Quieter Western Port beaches. Point Leo is patrolled in summer and sits next to the Point Leo Estate sculpture park.

## Walks and outdoor escapes

**Two Bays Walk.** 26km one-way from Dromana foreshore to Cape Schanck (Parks Victoria figure; Trail Hiking Australia and the Two Bays Trail Run commonly cite 28km — a discrepancy we cover in full in the section-by-section guide). Grade 3 moderate. Carry all your water — no drinking taps along the trail. Lightwood Creek camping area sits at the mid-point: five campsites, no fees, no bookings required, max two nights. → [Two Bays Walk guide](/outdoors/two-bays-walk/)

**Cape Schanck boardwalk.** 2.1km return from the carpark to Pulpit Rock and Pebble Beach; allow 45 minutes to an hour. The 1859 lighthouse is at the trailhead. Upgrades currently in progress — check on-site signage. → [Cape Schanck guide](/outdoors/cape-schanck/)

**Point Nepean National Park.** Entry is free. The Fort Nepean shuttle is around $12 return if you don't want to walk the full route out. Two all-terrain beach wheelchairs and one standard wheelchair are free to borrow from the Information Centre. → [Point Nepean guide](/outdoors/point-nepean/)

**Bushrangers Bay.** 5.4km return from the Cape Schanck eastern carpark, about two hours. Spectacular, unpatrolled, and not a swimming beach — Parks Victoria's own language includes "unpredictable rips, strong currents, rocky reefs, large waves" and "limited access for emergency services." Worth every step; not for a swim.

**Greens Bush.** The largest remnant of native bushland on the peninsula, tucked between Arthurs Seat and Cape Schanck. The Two Bays route passes straight through it. 8.9km of the trail runs inside Greens Bush, with circuit options at either end. Phone reception is unreliable — carry a printed map.

## Accessibility on the Peninsula's beaches

Five front beaches are fitted with beach matting from late November to the end of April or May:

- **Mt Martha Beach** — daily at the SLSC
- **Mothers Beach, Mornington** — daily
- **Rosebud Beach** — daily near the jetty
- **Rye Front Beach** — near the pier
- **Sorrento Front Beach** — near The Baths

Mount Martha has two floating MobiChairs available during weekend patrol hours, 12–5pm. They float — wheelchairs can enter the water. Phone (03) 5974 4140 to confirm.

On the ocean side, Sorrento Back Beach has a 40m accessible ramp (slope 1:12 then 1:14) near the kiosk — Parks Victoria's own wording calls it the best accessibility access point on the National Park's ocean beaches.

At Point Nepean, the Quarantine Station, Coles Track and Defence Road are accessible. The shuttle bus accommodates standard (not motorised) wheelchairs. Two all-terrain beach wheelchairs and one standard wheelchair are free to borrow from the Information Centre.

## Practical notes

**Parking.** Sorrento Back Beach has a large car park. Portsea Back has four. Gunnamatta has two large car parks off Truemans Road. Front beaches are metered or time-limited in peak season — check Shire signage.

**Best season.** Front beaches are excellent November through April. Ocean beaches are patrolled summer weekends and school holidays; outside that window, only experienced swimmers and surfers should enter the water. Walks are best outside the heat of summer — the Two Bays Walk is exposed.

**What to bring.** Water (always, especially on the Two Bays Walk — no taps). A printed map for Greens Bush — phone reception is unreliable. Sun protection. A rash top for snorkelling at Mushroom Reef or Rye Pier.

Two days is enough to see both coastlines. Morning at Sorrento Front, afternoon Cape Schanck boardwalk and Pebble Beach, dinner at the Flinders Hotel, dawn walk at Bushrangers Bay the next day.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which beach is best for kids?**
Mothers Beach in Mornington — sheltered by the pier, shallow, patrolled on select summer days, close to cafes and playgrounds. Safety Beach, Rye Front and Rosebud are also consistently calm. Avoid Bass Strait back beaches for young children.

**Where can I take my dog?**
Year-round off-leash: Tassells Cove (Safety Beach), Hawker Beach (Mount Martha), Fossil Beach (Mornington), Flinders Beach north end, Somers Beach (with time variations), Anthony's Nose (Dromana). Nowhere in the Mornington Peninsula National Park — every ocean back beach is included in that prohibition. Shire bay beaches allow on-leash dogs, but with a 9am–7pm sand prohibition during daylight saving.

**What's the difference between a front beach and a back beach?**
Front beaches face Port Phillip Bay — calm, shallow, close to the towns. Back beaches face Bass Strait — open ocean, surf, cliffs, strong rip currents. Front is for swimming and paddling; back is for surfing and cliff-top walks.

**Is the Two Bays Walk hard?**
Grade 3 moderate — doable in a long day for fit walkers, or split across two days with a night at Lightwood Creek. The track is 26km one-way from Dromana to Cape Schanck. Harder sections: the climb up Arthurs Seat and the final descent to Cape Schanck. Greens Bush in the middle is relatively flat. Carry all your water.
