Most visitors arrive on the Mornington Peninsula for beaches and wine. They leave having also done one of Australasia's better cable car rides, visited a wildlife sanctuary with free-roaming kangaroos, or soaked in geothermal water drawn from 637 metres underground.

The Peninsula's activity spread is broader than the marketing suggests. What follows is a map of what's actually here, built from primary sources.

One thing worth leading with: **Ashcombe Maze is open**. Multiple travel sites incorrectly list it as closed following a period of reduced operation around 2020. The café re-opened in January 2025 and the maze is fully trading Thursday to Monday. It's Australia's oldest traditional hedge maze, and the lavender labyrinth alone justifies the drive to Shoreham.

---

## Thermal springs

Two separate geothermal spring complexes sit five minutes apart near Rye. They are not the same experience.

### Peninsula Hot Springs

The original, opened 2005, drawing from a 637m underground aquifer. The Bath House has 70+ experiences and is open to all ages — it's the more communal, accessible complex. The Spa Dreaming Centre is adults-only (16+) with a quieter premium atmosphere.

Pricing is not published on the site. Earlybird weekday entry is consistently the best-value and least-crowded option. The main known complaint is overcrowding in the Bath House during peak periods. If visiting on a Saturday, book in advance and arrive at opening.

→ [Full comparison: Peninsula Hot Springs vs Alba](/journal/peninsula-hot-springs-vs-alba/)

### Alba Thermal Springs & Spa

Adults-only (18+). Brutalist architecture, 22 geothermal pools (some sources cite 31 — 22 is confirmed on the primary site), a restaurant by Karen Martini, and an atmosphere that leans boutique rather than resort. Night Owl sessions are particularly good value.

The Sanctuary villas opened in 2025: five hilltop villas plus two premium rooms, adults-only, from $624/night.

## Arthurs Seat Eagle

The cable car opened in December 2016, running 24 gondolas along a ~1km cable from the valley floor to the highest point on the Peninsula (314 metres). Each gondola seats 8 and is wheelchair and pram accessible. Companion Card holders admitted free.

Adult return: **$32.50** (some older sources cite $30.90 — that figure is outdated). Child return: $21.00. Family: $89.00.

Standard hours: Mon–Fri 10am–5pm, Sat–Sun 10am–6pm, last boarding 4:30pm. Extended in school and public holidays — verify before visiting.

The **Enchanted Adventure Garden** is on the same hill: 25 acres with four hedge mazes (two hedge, one turf, one children's), five giant tube slides, a canopy walk, and 20+ themed gardens. Adult entry $40, children $30. Open Sep–Apr 9am–5pm, May–Aug 10am–5pm.

## Wildlife

### Moonlit Sanctuary Wildlife Conservation Park

TripAdvisor's #1 attraction on the Mornington Peninsula (4.6 stars, 741 reviews, Travellers' Choice 2025) — yet receives almost no editorial coverage. Located at the northern tip of the Peninsula near Pearcedale, 50 minutes from Melbourne.

60+ Australian native species, including 80+ free-roaming kangaroos and wallabies, and one of Victoria's only captive breeding programs for the critically endangered orange-bellied parrot.

Open daily 9:30am–4pm (last entry 3pm). Adult admission approximately $25–29 — verify directly before visiting, as the primary site blocks external verification. Night tours available and must be pre-booked.

### Polperro Dolphin Swims

September to May, from Sorrento Pier. Two departures daily: 8am and 12pm. Tours run 3–4 hours. TripAdvisor's #1 outdoor activity in Sorrento (4.8 stars, 306 reviews). Swim tours from approximately $138 adult; sightseeing cruise from approximately $72 adult (OTA 2025 figures — verify with Polperro directly).

Family operation established in 1986 by the Muir family. Victorian Tourism Awards Hall of Fame for environmental tourism since 2001.

## The maze

### Ashcombe Maze & Lavender Gardens

**Open. Thursday to Monday, 10am–5pm.** Closed Tuesday and Wednesday.

Multiple travel websites still list Ashcombe as closed. This is incorrect. The café re-opened January 2025 and the full maze and gardens are operating. Closed Tuesday and Wednesday year-round, open on public holidays and school holidays.

Australia's oldest traditional hedge maze: more than 1,000 *Cupressus macrocarpa* trees, clipped three times a year, growing over 3 metres high. The lavender labyrinth uses 4,000+ plants across 40+ varieties. Entry from $15 per person — verify full price breakdown at ashcombemaze.com.au. Allow 1.5–2 hours. Shoreham, 15 Shoreham Road.

## Free and low-cost

**Coolart Wetlands & Homestead** (Somers): Completely free. Daily 9am–5pm, closed Christmas Day. Wheelchair accessible; wheelchairs available to borrow from the Visitor Centre. Birdwatching, heritage homestead, wetlands walk.

**Point Nepean National Park** (Portsea): Free vehicle and pedestrian entry. Fort Nepean is approximately 3km on foot from Gunner's Cottage carpark, or take the hop-on hop-off shuttle (~$12 adult return). 50+ heritage-listed buildings. Two all-terrain beach wheelchairs and one standard wheelchair free to borrow from the Information Centre.

→ [Point Nepean National Park guide](/journal/point-nepean-national-park-guide/)

**Cape Schanck Lighthouse** (Cape Schanck): Lighthouse grounds free (6am–6pm daily — verify Parks Victoria). The 1859 limestone lighthouse and boardwalk to Pulpit Rock are the highlights. Lighthouse tours are ticketed: approximately $16–17 adult on weekends and school/public holidays.

<AlertBlock type="warning" heading="Cape Schanck boardwalk — works in progress">
Parks Victoria is completing upgrades to the visitor precinct. Track resurfacing, lookout improvements, and boardwalk works may affect some sections. Follow on-site signage. Works expected complete late April 2026 — verify before visiting.
</AlertBlock>

**Pt. Leo Estate Sculpture Park** (Merricks): $15 adult, children under 12 free. 330 acres with works by Yayoi Kusama, KAWS, and rotating international exhibitions. Daily 11am–5pm (last entry 4:30pm).

## Seasonal

**Sunny Ridge Strawberry Farm** (Main Ridge): U-pick season runs November to April for strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries. Over 60 years in operation; distributes to Coles, Woolworths, and Aldi nationally.

**Searoad Ferries** (Sorrento–Queenscliff): The 40-minute ferry crossing isn't an attraction in itself, but it's a natural addition to any circuit of the western tip of the Peninsula. Pets are welcome on board. Book ahead in summer.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Ashcombe Maze open?**
Yes. Confirmed open Thursday to Monday, 10am–5pm. Café re-opened January 2025. Closed Tuesday and Wednesday.

**Is Peninsula Hot Springs worth the money?**
On a quiet weekday, yes — one of the better thermal bathing experiences in Victoria. On a peak summer weekend in the Bath House, it can feel crowded. Book an earlybird midweek slot if you can. Adults without children should consider Alba Thermal Springs — smaller, adults-only (18+), and a different atmosphere.

**What can you do for free on the Mornington Peninsula?**
Point Nepean National Park (free entry; shuttle optional), Coolart Wetlands & Homestead (free), Cape Schanck lighthouse grounds (free; tours ticketed), and the ocean beaches in the National Park.

**What's best to do with kids?**
Enchanted Adventure Garden for older children (mazes, tube slides, tree surfing). Sunny Ridge Strawberry Farm for U-pick from November. Moonlit Sanctuary for animals at any age. Front beaches — Mornington Mothers Beach, Rye, Rosebud — for toddlers.
