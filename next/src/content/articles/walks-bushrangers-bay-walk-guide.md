Bushrangers Bay is one of the most satisfying walks on the Mornington Peninsula because it feels wilder than the drive suggests. You leave the lighthouse precinct at Cape Schanck, head down through coastal scrub, and finish at a broad crescent of sand and basalt that looks like it belongs much further from Melbourne than it does.

It is not a secret in the strict sense, but it still feels like one if you time it well. Go early, bring water, and treat the return climb with respect in warm weather.

## The short version

**Distance:** 5km return  
**Duration:** 1.5 to 2 hours return  
**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**Terrain:** coastal track, steps, uneven ground, beach section  
**Dogs:** Not permitted  
**Start point:** Cape Schanck Lighthouse car park  
**Best season:** autumn, winter, spring  
**Best time of day:** morning or late afternoon

## Why this walk is worth doing

This is one of the Peninsula walks that actually delivers on the build-up. The descent gives you open coastal views quickly, the track feels substantial without being punishing, and the payoff at the bay feels dramatic rather than token.

You get:
- a proper sense of cliffs and weather
- a beach that feels genuinely removed from the suburban Peninsula
- enough effort to make it memorable
- a clear pairing with Cape Schanck, Flinders, or a Hot Springs afternoon

## What the walk is really like

The first part is straightforward: a clear path leaving the Cape Schanck area and dropping gradually through coastal vegetation. It is easy to start too fast here because the track looks gentle. Save a bit for the return.

As you descend, the sea opens up properly and the bay starts to reveal itself. The final approach is where the walk becomes more cinematic, with the cliffs, rocks, and broader shape of the coastline doing the work.

At the bottom, Bushrangers Bay feels much less managed than some of the Peninsula’s more polished foreshore stops. That is the appeal. It is a place to stop, look, catch your breath, and decide whether you want a short rest or a longer one before climbing back out.

## What catches people out

This is not a difficult hike by serious hiking standards, but it is hard enough to expose poor planning.

The common mistakes are:
- starting late in the day with not enough water
- underestimating the return climb
- wearing poor shoes because the route is “only” 5km
- assuming the beach means a casual swim-and-walk setup

The walk is best treated as a coastal hike with a beach payoff, not as a beach trip with a short walk attached.

## Who this walk suits

**Best for:**
- visitors who want one big scenic Peninsula walk
- couples building a Cape Schanck day
- active families with older kids
- photographers in softer light

**Less suited to:**
- prams
- very young children
- people wanting a flat recovery stroll
- anyone expecting dog access

## Best pairings after the walk

This is where Peninsula Insider can make the day better.

### Walk + Hot Springs
Bushrangers Bay in the morning, Peninsula Hot Springs in the afternoon, is one of the strongest Peninsula combinations. Do the walk first while you still have energy, then head north to soak.

### Walk + Flinders lunch
If you want something more food-led afterwards, push south-west toward Flinders and make the walk the dramatic opening rather than the whole day.

### Walk + Cape Schanck short extension
If the group still has energy, add the boardwalk or lighthouse precinct rather than trying to force another full hike.

## Practical notes

- Check weather before you go. Wind changes the feel of this walk quickly.
- Mobile coverage is not something to rely on completely.
- There is limited shade, so sun matters.
- National park rules apply, including dog restrictions.
- If it has rained, parts of the track can feel looser and less forgiving underfoot.

## Final read

If you only do one dramatic southern Peninsula walk, Bushrangers Bay has a strong case. It is long enough to feel like an outing, scenic enough to justify the drive, and flexible enough to pair with a bigger Peninsula day.

It is not the easiest walk on the site, and that is part of why it stays memorable.
