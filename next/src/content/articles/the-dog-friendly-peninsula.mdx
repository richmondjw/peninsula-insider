The Peninsula is one of the most dog-friendly regions in Australia, and it is also the region where dog-weekend planning most often goes wrong.

The reasons are structural. Melbourne has the second-highest dog-ownership rate in Australia, most Peninsula weekends are built around food and wine, and most food-and-wine spots are technically dog-friendly in their outdoor areas only. The trap is simple: you book a table at a cellar door, the weather turns, and you are outside in a rainstorm with a wet retriever while everyone else has moved inside. Or you drive to the back beach at Sorrento with a dog that has been in the car for two hours, and discover that those beaches are inside the National Park — where dogs are not permitted.

None of these problems are unsolvable. They are just problems no one tells you about in advance. Here is the actual guide.

## The principle: build the day around the dog, not the dog around the day

The single biggest mistake dog-weekend visitors make is fitting the dog into the standard weekend plan. That weekend has a cellar-door lunch, a back-beach walk, a village stroll, and a pub dinner, and it assumes the dog is happy in the car between each stop. Six hours of driving, one hour of walking, cellar door with the dog under the table, a late ferry, and a tired animal by five.

It does not work. Dogs on weekends need more total walking than that plan gives them, and less total sitting-in-a-dining-room time. The version that works inverts the ratio: two proper walks a day, two or three meals that are short and easy, and a base close enough to the walks that the dog is not getting car-sick every time you move.

## Where to stay with the dog

The first decision is the base, and it matters more with a dog than without.

**Dog-friendly stays with outdoor space and no stairs.** The strongest Peninsula dog stays are holiday houses, cottages, and self-contained accommodation rather than traditional hotel inventory. Owners should verify fencing, indoor rules, bedding and furniture rules, and any pet fees before committing. The pet-friendly options sell out faster than standard rooms — book early.

**What to avoid.** Hotels with carpeted corridors and a small "pet-friendly" wing. Dogs do not cope well with stairs and lifts in unfamiliar buildings, and the distance from room to grass becomes the whole logistics problem of the weekend.

## The Peninsula's best dog beaches

The confirmed year-round off-leash beaches are:

- **Tassells Cove** (Safety Beach) — calm bay, easy access, low drama
- **Hawker Beach** (Mount Martha) — confirm you are at the right section; Mount Martha South and the main beach have different rules
- **Fossil Beach** (Mornington) — quieter; clifftop access; named for the marine fossils in the rock face
- **Somers Beach** east of the yacht club — broad foreshore, less visited
- **Flinders Beach north end** (between Dodds Creek and Manton Creek) — Western Port side of Flinders village; not to be confused with Flinders Ocean Beach, which is inside the National Park and prohibits dogs
- **Anthony's Nose** (Dromana) — rocky foreshore character

On most other bay-side beaches, the **9am–7pm daylight saving prohibition** applies (roughly November to April). Early morning or after-7pm access is usually possible — but arrive and read the signage, as zone rules are updated seasonally.

**The National Park is a hard no.** Dogs are not permitted anywhere in the Mornington Peninsula National Park. This is not a seasonal rule. It includes:

- All ocean beaches: Sorrento Back Beach, Portsea Back Beach, Gunnamatta, Rye Ocean Beach, Flinders Ocean Beach
- Cape Schanck and its walking tracks
- Bushrangers Bay walk
- Greens Bush
- Point Nepean National Park

The PAPP baiting programme currently active in this area makes this a life-safety issue — see the alert at the top of this page.

## The vineyards and breweries that actually welcome dogs

**Stonier Wines** (Merricks) — lawn is dog-friendly, confirmed on venue site. Walk-in friendly.

**Main Ridge Estate** (Red Hill) — on-lead only; must pre-book and tick the dog box. Outdoor Larder restaurant. The most serious wine on the Peninsula if you want Half Acre Pinot Noir.

**Green Olive at Red Hill** (Red Hill South) — outdoor dining areas confirmed dog-friendly.

**Many Little Bar & Dining** (Red Hill South) — outdoor seating, enquire on booking. Hatted kitchen.

**Red Hill Brewery** — grass tables, wood-fired pizza, outdoor setting. Dogs are the expected mode rather than a negotiation.

**St Andrews Beach Brewery** — converted horse stable, large grassed area. One of the better outdoor dog environments on the Peninsula.

**Foxeys Hangout** says "please leave your lovely dogs at home." **Quealy Winemakers** asks "no dogs." Respect both.

## The pubs

**Flinders Hotel** — beer garden welcomes dogs. Food is a cut above most Peninsula pubs. Village setting means you can park once, eat, and walk the dog around Flinders before driving anywhere.

**Portsea Hotel** — beer garden dog-friendly. Bay views. Walk-in only peak season; bookings accepted rest of year.

**Balnarring Pub** — outdoor beer garden dog-welcome, local atmosphere.

**The Rocks at Mornington** — outdoor deck dog-friendly. Waterfront marina views.

## A proper dog weekend plan

For a couple with a medium-sized dog, arriving Friday afternoon:

### Friday
- **3.30pm** — Check in. Walk the dog around the property.
- **4.30pm** — Drive to Tassells Cove (Safety Beach) or Somers Beach for a long off-leash run.
- **6pm** — Back to base. Cold drink, feed the dog.
- **7pm** — Dinner at Balnarring Pub, outdoor beer garden.

### Saturday
- **7.30am** — Early walk before 9am while the beach restriction window is clear.
- **9am** — Mornington Farmers Market if it is the second Saturday of the month (dogs on-leash, outdoor market grounds).
- **11am** — Coffee at Commonfolk (Mornington, outdoor courtyard) or Georgie Bass (Flinders, outdoor seating).
- **12.30pm** — Lunch at Red Hill Brewery or Stonier Wines. Budget two hours.
- **3pm** — Return to base.
- **5pm** — Late afternoon walk on a council foreshore track.
- **7pm** — Easy dinner close to base.

### Sunday
- **8am** — Long walk at your best dog beach. Fossil Beach for something different.
- **10am** — Coffee with outdoor tables.
- **11.30am** — Flinders Hotel beer garden for an early lunch.
- **1pm** — Drive home beating Sunday traffic.

## What to avoid

- **National Park sections.** Cape Schanck, Bushrangers Bay, Portsea and Sorrento back beaches, Point Nepean, Greens Bush. All hard prohibited, with PAPP baiting active until October 2026.
- **Summer front beaches between 9am and 7pm.** Dog access is restricted during daylight saving.
- **A standard cellar-door-to-cellar-door day without checking policy.** Most Red Hill cellar doors have unclear or no-dog policies. Stick to the confirmed list.
- **Cars in summer heat.** Heat stress happens fast.
