The Mornington Peninsula's best-known experiences — Peninsula Hot Springs, Arthurs Seat Eagle, Ashcombe Maze, the hatted restaurants — all cost money. But there are genuinely good free options, and several of them rank among the best the Peninsula offers.

---

## Completely free

### Coolart Wetlands & Homestead — Somers
The most underrated free stop on the Peninsula. A heritage homestead dating to the 1890s, set within extensive wetlands and birdwatching grounds on the Westernport side of the Peninsula at Somers. Wheelchair accessible; wheelchairs available to borrow from the Visitor Centre.

**Entry:** Free. **Hours:** Daily 9am–5pm, gates close 5pm sharp. Closed Christmas Day. No booking required.

Lord Somers Road, Somers. [coolart.org.au](https://coolart.org.au)

### Point Nepean National Park — Portsea
The western tip of the Peninsula costs nothing to enter. Vehicle and pedestrian entry is free. Inside the park: over 50 heritage-listed buildings including Fort Nepean, a working quarantine station history, bay swimming at the Quarantine Station beach, and views out across the Heads.

Fort Nepean is approximately 3km on foot from Gunner's Cottage carpark. If you prefer not to walk, the hop-on hop-off internal shuttle runs approximately $12 adult return. Two all-terrain beach wheelchairs and one standard wheelchair are available free from the Information Centre.

**Entry:** Free. Vehicles: 8am–6pm (8am–8pm daylight saving). Pedestrians: any time.

→ [Point Nepean National Park guide](/journal/point-nepean-national-park-guide/)

### Cape Schanck Lighthouse Grounds — Cape Schanck
The 1859 limestone lighthouse and its surrounding grounds are free to access from 6am (verify current hours with Parks Victoria). The boardwalk walk to Pulpit Rock and the basalt clifftop views are among the best on the Peninsula — and they cost nothing. Lighthouse tours are ticketed (~$16–17 adult on weekends and school holidays), but the walk and views don't require a tour.

<AlertBlock type="warning" heading="Boardwalk works in progress">
Parks Victoria is completing upgrades to the Cape Schanck visitor precinct. Track resurfacing, lookout improvements, and boardwalk works may affect some sections. Follow on-site signage. Works expected complete late April 2026 — verify before visiting.
</AlertBlock>

### National Park ocean beaches — by foot
All ocean beaches within the Mornington Peninsula National Park are free to access on foot: Sorrento Back Beach, Portsea Back Beach, Gunnamatta, Rye Ocean Beach. Note that dogs are not permitted anywhere in the National Park.

**Important:** PAPP fox baiting and trapping is active from Portsea Ocean Beach to Greens Bush until October 2026. Do not take dogs to any National Park area.

### Foreshore walks — bay beaches
The foreshore paths along the Port Phillip Bay side of the Peninsula are free: Sorrento to Blairgowrie, Rye to Rosebud, the Mornington harbour walk. None of these require paid entry.

## Low cost (under $20)

### Pt. Leo Estate Sculpture Park — Merricks ($15 adult, children under 12 free)
330 acres of manicured gardens with permanent works by Yayoi Kusama and KAWS and rotating international exhibitions. One of the most serious sculpture parks in Australia, on a working vineyard. The entry fee is $15 adult — children under 12 are free.

Daily 11am–5pm, last entry 4:30pm. 3649 Frankston-Flinders Road, Merricks.

### Mornington Peninsula Regional Gallery — Mornington (free)
The regional gallery at Mornington runs changing exhibitions and a permanent collection with no entry charge. Hours vary by exhibition — check before visiting.

### Bushrangers Bay Walk — Cape Schanck (free)
The 5.4km return walk from the Cape Schanck eastern carpark to Bushrangers Bay is free. Ocean views, basalt coastline, coastal heathland. Allow approximately two hours return. Note: dogs not permitted (MPNP). Check Parks Victoria for current access — possible secondary closures due to fox control activity.

## Seasonal free events

**Mornington Peninsula Pinot Noir Celebration** — typically March; ticketed tastings but free supporting events. Check event calendar.

**Red Hill Market** — first Saturday of the month (except January), Red Hill Showgrounds. Entry free; produce, art, craft stalls.

**Farmers markets** — Mornington, Red Hill, Dromana, and Flinders all run regular farmers markets. Check local schedules for exact dates and entry costs.

---

## A free day itinerary

Start early at Coolart Wetlands (free; 9am opening) for an hour of birdwatching. Drive south to Cape Schanck — park in the lighthouse carpark (free) and walk the boardwalk to Pulpit Rock. Pack lunch and eat on the headland. Afternoon: drive to Portsea and enter Point Nepean National Park (free) — walk to Fort Nepean or take the shuttle. Sunset drive back along Point Nepean Road. Total cost: $0 (or ~$24 adult if you take the shuttle and do the lighthouse tour).
