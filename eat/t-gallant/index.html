T'Gallant

The Peninsula's Pinot Grigio pioneer - a sprawling, family-friendly winery with a wood-fired Italian trattoria (La Baracca) at its heart

Book a tasting +61 3 5989 6565
T'Gallant · Main Ridge

T'Gallant essentially invented Australian Pinot Grigio as a commercial category - Kathleen Quealy and Kevin McCarthy planted the first vines in the late 1980s and spent years convincing everyone else that Italian varieties belonged on the Peninsula. They went on to found Quealy; T'Gallant continued under the Treasury Wine Estates umbrella and is now the most reliably family-friendly cellar door on the Main Ridge.

La Baracca, the on-site wood-fired trattoria, is the easy answer when you have kids, grandparents, and a wine-curious cousin all in the same car: wood-fired pizzas, handmade pasta, a short carafe list led by the estate's Pinot Grigio, and a huge shaded outdoor area where nobody minds if the kids run between the vines. The tasting room is open daily and the flights move briskly.

Come for Sunday lunch, order the Prosecco and the margherita, and don't try to make it more than it is. T'Gallant is the Peninsula at its most unserious, and that is exactly its purpose.
