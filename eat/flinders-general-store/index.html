Flinders General Store

The village general store on Flinders' main street - slow-drip coffee, fresh bread, pick-your-own picnics for the Blowhole walk

Flinders General Store · Flinders

Flinders General Store is the reason Flinders Village still feels like a village. The building is a proper country general store - timber floors, high shelves, a bell on the door - and it holds the things you actually need for a weekend down here: slow-drip coffee, a daily loaf from the on-site bakery, proper breakfast sandwiches to eat in or take to the Blowhole walk, and a small grocery shelf of Peninsula pantry essentials.

Use it as your morning coffee stop on a Flinders weekend. Use it as your picnic outfitter before the walk to West Head. Use it as the casual lunch option when Pier Street is booked out. The team runs the place with unfussy warmth and the pastry cabinet - lemon slice, custard tarts, an honest sausage roll - is better than it needs to be.

The proper working heart of the village.
