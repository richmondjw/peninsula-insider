Allis Wine Bar

The low-waste, low-key sibling of Ten Minutes by Tractor's fine dining room — small plates, garden-harvested produce, and a thoughtful wine list

Allis Wine Bar · Main Ridge

Allis is the casual-hearted sibling to Ten Minutes by Tractor's fine dining room, and for a lot of people who know the Peninsula well it is quietly the better meal of the two. The format is small-plate and low-waste: vegetables straight out of the Ten Minutes kitchen garden, offcuts from the main restaurant deliberately integrated into the menu, and a wine list built to drink rather than to study.

The room is deliberately relaxed — a wine bar rather than a dining room — and the point is the honest exchange between the kitchen and the produce on hand. Go in solo for a glass and two plates at the counter. Go as a couple for a slow dinner on a quiet weeknight. Either way, the cooking rewards attention without demanding it.

An underrated Peninsula room and a genuine find for anyone who thinks they already know the ridge.
