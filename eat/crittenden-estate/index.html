Crittenden Estate

The Peninsula's pioneering estate, now pouring Pinot, Chardonnay and the Spanish-leaning Los Hermanos range from a new lakeside wine centre

Book a tasting +61 3 5981 8322
Crittenden Estate · Dromana

Crittenden is one of the founding estates of the Peninsula wine region - Garry Crittenden planted the first vines in 1982 and the property has stayed in the family for four decades, now under second-generation winemaker Rollo. That history matters, but it is not why you drive here. You drive here because the Crittenden line-up is one of the few on the Peninsula that genuinely surprises people: alongside textbook Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, Rollo has built out the Los Hermanos range - Savagnin, Arneis, Nebbiolo - working with Spanish and Italian varieties that barely exist anywhere else in the region.

The new Wine Centre, completed recently, sits beside the lake at the heart of the estate and is designed for proper seated tastings rather than elbow-to-elbow counter pours. Pair a flight with the lakeside Stillwater restaurant next door for one of the most complete half-days in Dromana. Open daily for tastings; book for the restaurant.

Ask the team to walk you through the skin-contact and amphora-aged experiments - it is where the second generation is clearly stretching out.
