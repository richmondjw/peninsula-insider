Walk-In Restaurants on the Mornington Peninsula

Most serious Peninsula restaurants require bookings — often weeks or months in advance for hatted venues. But there are genuine walk-in options, and two of them are hatted: Rare Hare at Willow Creek takes no reservations at all and is walk-in only from 2pm on weekends; Foxeys Hangout accepts no bookings and has a maximum group size of 6.

Below the hatted tier, Montalto Piazza (daily, 11am–5pm), most of the Peninsula's village cafes and bakeries, and most pub bistros operate on a walk-in basis. If you have arrived on the Peninsula without a booking for the destination you wanted, this list is where to start.

Frequently asked questions

Which restaurants do not require a booking?

Rare Hare (Merricks North): walk-in only, no reservations, from 2pm weekends. Foxeys Hangout (Red Hill): no bookings, max group 6. Montalto Piazza: walk-in daily 11am–5pm. Most cafes and pub bistros also operate walk-in.

Can I walk in to a hatted restaurant?

Rare Hare (one hat) and Many Little (one hat, some sessions) are the only hatted walk-in options. All other hatted Peninsula restaurants require advance booking.

What if I haven't booked?

Try Rare Hare, Foxeys Hangout, or Montalto Piazza. Village cafes and bakeries are almost always walk-in. Pub bistros usually have midweek capacity.
