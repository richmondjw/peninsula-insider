Peninsula This Weekend

18 to 20 April

The weekend of the Mornington Cup, the last real warmth before Easter, and a second-act Sunday that’s cheaper than it looks.

◆ The pick

Mornington Cup Raceday

Saturday 18 April

Mornington

Worth the drive

The one day the town collectively takes the afternoon off. Park at Mornington village, walk in through the gardens (25 minutes), arrive by noon, eat before race three. The 4pm light on the grass is the photograph you take home.

After: Dinner at Allis Wine Bar or Dromana Hotel. Both take walk-ins on race day if you arrive by 7.

Note: Adults day. Kids will tolerate it; they won’t enjoy it.

◇ If the weather turns

The three-move rainy day

8:45 AM

Red Hill Market

Covered stalls. Arrive early, eat a fritter, buy olive oil, leave by 10:30.

1:00 PM

Alba Thermal Springs

Rain-proof recovery. The quieter thermal complex with dedicated silent sessions. Book the morning circuit, eat on the ridge after.

3:30 PM

Long lunch on the ridge

Quealy Winemakers for wine-bar pace, or Ten Minutes by Tractor if the day has earned a proper sit-down.

✕ Skip this

The generic school-holiday event blitz

Every venue on the Peninsula is running some version of “Easter family fun” this fortnight. Most are filler. If you’re travelling with kids, pick exactly one:

Moonlit Sanctuary

Twilight walk. Book the kids — they’ll remember it forever. Kids Grade A

Sunny Ridge Strawberry Farm

Pick, pay, picnic, walk. Sunny days only. Kids Grade A

Briars Eco Explorers

Free. Council-run. One of the best-hidden family outings on the Peninsula. Kids Grade A
