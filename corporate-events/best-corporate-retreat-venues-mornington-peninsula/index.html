Best Retreat Venues

Best Corporate Retreat Venues on the Mornington Peninsula

A corporate retreat venue on the Peninsula should do more than look good in photos. It should change the quality of the conversations. This guide matches venues to group size, event format, and what each property actually delivers — not what the marketing suggests.

Corporate Retreats · Jackalope · Peninsula

How this guide works

Start with the format. Then choose the venue.

The most common retreat planning mistake is choosing the venue before deciding the format. A beautiful vineyard property that is optimised for intimate dinners is not the same as a strong executive retreat venue. A conference centre with resort infrastructure is not a leadership offsite venue. These distinctions matter and most venue comparison guides ignore them.

This guide structures venues by use case — small executive retreats, medium offsites, large conference events, and winery-led programmes. Match your format first, then choose within the relevant category.

6–20 people

Executive retreat venues

Small senior-team gatherings where the venue character is the programme. Boutique properties with privacy, distinctive atmosphere, and a dining programme that does not require an external caterer.

Boutique · Merricks North

Jackalope

The Peninsula's most architecturally distinctive boutique hotel — a black monolith in a vineyard, with a hatted restaurant (Doot Doot Doot) and a spa onsite. Suited to small executive teams where the visual context of the event matters and the group is happy to stay within the property ecosystem for two days. Does well: design distinctiveness, exceptional food and wine, onsite spa, sense of arrival. Watch out for: the architecture is bold — not every group finds it relaxing. Limited capacity. Not suitable for groups over 30.

Retreat hotel · Red Hill

Lancemore Lindenderry

A retreat hotel on the Red Hill plateau with vineyard surroundings, onsite rooms and suites, a strong restaurant programme, and event spaces suited to leadership sessions and strategy days. Less architecturally assertive than Jackalope — better for groups that want quality without theatre. Does well: accommodation integration, hinterland atmosphere, reliable dining. Watch out for: the event infrastructure is functional but not purpose-designed for large or complex programmes.

Red Hill guide →
Private · Flinders / Shoreham corridor

Boutique hinterland properties

For retreats where focus matters more than amenity. The Flinders and Shoreham corridor has private properties available for exclusive-use bookings — suited to boards, founding teams, and senior groups that want to be genuinely unreachable from the office for 48 hours. Does well: privacy, distance, authenticity. Watch out for: limited inventory; availability and AV infrastructure vary significantly by property. Requires earlier planning than name-brand venues.

Flinders guide →

15–60 people

Leadership offsite venues

Mixed-format retreats that need structured session space, accommodation within 10 minutes, and an evening programme that does not require a coach between venues.

Coastal hotel · Sorrento

The Continental Sorrento

A full-service coastal hotel with conference capacity, onsite accommodation, a precinct of restaurants, and direct foreshore access. The strongest Peninsula option for groups that want everything walkable and a town atmosphere after the sessions end. Does well: walkability, group accommodation under one roof, town-centre evening energy. Watch out for: Sorrento is busy in summer; the town atmosphere can cut both ways depending on the group.

Sorrento guide →
Winery · Red Hill

Montalto

Up to 120 guests for a vineyard-led leadership event. The dining programme is the centrepiece — a working lunch or private dinner at Montalto does more for group cohesion than most facilitated activities. Does well: food and wine quality, estate atmosphere, sculpture garden for post-session walking. Watch out for: AV and formal session infrastructure is not the strength; better suited to programme formats where dining is the main structured activity.

Red Hill guide →

40+ people

Conference and large-group venues

Events that need delegate infrastructure — structured AV, breakout rooms, accommodation blocks, and catering at scale.

Resort · Cape Schanck

RACV Cape Schanck Resort

The Peninsula's largest conference venue — accommodation, multiple meeting rooms with AV infrastructure, golf, and a spa all on one property. The right choice when group size, logistical containment, and activities variety are the primary requirements. Does well: scale, accommodation density, multi-activity programme integration. Watch out for: feels less boutique than the Red Hill or Sorrento options — better for logistics than for intimate retreat atmosphere.

Cape Schanck guide →
Coastal hotel · Sorrento

The Continental Sorrento

Also the Peninsula's strongest large-group option outside RACV Cape Schanck — up to 200 for events, hotel accommodation onsite, and a town precinct that gives delegates genuine evening options without a coach. The atmosphere is more curated than a resort; better when the group quality is the priority. Does well: coastal hotel atmosphere, delegate walkability, strong evening programme. Watch out for: conference-scale events here can feel crowded in the available session spaces; verify breakout room availability explicitly.

Common questions

What planners actually ask

Red Hill or Sorrento for a corporate offsite?

Red Hill for strategy-heavy retreats where food and wine are central and you want immersion. Sorrento for polished coastal hosting, client-facing events, and groups where walkability and a recognisable town format matter. Cape Schanck when group size or multi-activity logistics are the primary consideration. → Full comparison →

How far in advance should I book a Peninsula retreat venue?

For peak-season weekends at boutique properties — Jackalope, Lindenderry — book 3 to 6 months ahead. For RACV Cape Schanck or The Continental for large group dates, 2 to 4 months is usually sufficient outside peak. For the most in-demand properties, longer. If you have a fixed event date, confirm venue availability before committing it to participants.

Is Peninsula Hot Springs usable as a retreat activity?

Yes — Peninsula Hot Springs is 25 minutes from Cape Schanck and easily bookable as a group activity. It works best as an evening recovery session on the first day of a two-day retreat, or as a second-morning activity before the drive back. Pre-book group sessions in advance — it fills on weekends and the best private pool options require lead time. → Hot springs guide
