## 1. Executive Summary

Peninsula Insider should not be run like a generic travel SEO project.

It is not trying to become a tourism-board clone, a directory at scale, or a keyword farm for destination terms. It is building toward a much more defensible position: **the definitive, editorially trusted, structurally useful guide to the Mornington Peninsula**.

That means search strategy must do two jobs at once:

1. grow organic visibility across the Peninsula’s major intent clusters
2. protect and strengthen the brand’s differentiator: taste, trust, local intelligence, and practical judgement

The central strategic insight is this:

**Peninsula Insider does not have a sections problem. It has an authority-distribution problem.**

The site already has a strong top-level structure:
- Eat
- Stay
- Wine
- Explore
- Escape
- What’s On
- Journal
- Places
- Golf
- Spa
- Dog-Friendly

But these sections do not yet consistently own their search intent. Too much authority is isolated in individual pages rather than compounding through hub pages, place pages, and tightly governed supporting content.

The role of this operating model is to fix that.

## 3. Brand-Protected SEO Principles

These principles are non-negotiable.

### 3.1 Editorial authority first
Every SEO page must still feel like a legitimate Peninsula Insider page.

If a page would feel embarrassing as editorial, it should not be published just because it could rank.

### 3.2 No brochure language
Do not publish pages built on generic tourism-board phrasing.

Avoid:
- world-class dining
- charming coastal villages
- rugged coastline
- unspoiled beaches
- generic lifestyle filler

Search growth must come from useful specificity, not cliché.

### 3.3 No thin keyword variants
Do not multiply pages simply to catch slightly different phrases unless the intent is materially different.

Bad example:
- best Mornington Peninsula restaurants
- best places to eat Mornington Peninsula
- top dining Mornington Peninsula

Good example:
- best restaurants Mornington Peninsula
- long lunch Mornington Peninsula
- where to eat in Red Hill

### 3.4 “Best of” pages must be defensible
If a page uses ranking or recommendation language, it must reflect actual editorial judgement, not a soft directory dump.

### 3.5 Search supports the brand, not the other way around
SEO is here to scale Peninsula Insider’s authority. It is not here to rewrite the publication into something flatter or more generic.

## 5. Search Intent Ownership Model

This is one of the most important parts of the system.

Each section must own a primary intent set.

### 5.1 Section ownership rules

#### /explore/
Owns:
- broad things-to-do intent
- beaches
- walks
- lookouts
- attractions
- activity-led discovery

Examples:
- things to do Mornington Peninsula
- best walks Mornington Peninsula
- beaches Mornington Peninsula

#### /stay/
Owns:
- accommodation intent
- where to stay intent
- best accommodation intent
- place-based stay selection
- stay modifiers (luxury, spa, couples, dog-friendly stay variants where applicable)

Examples:
- where to stay Mornington Peninsula
- best accommodation Mornington Peninsula
- luxury accommodation Mornington Peninsula

#### /eat/
Owns:
- restaurants
- cafés
- food discovery
- long lunch / brunch / dining planning intent

Examples:
- best restaurants Mornington Peninsula
- long lunch Mornington Peninsula
- where to eat Mornington Peninsula

#### /wine/
Owns:
- winery and cellar-door intent
- tasting and wine-region decision content
- winery lunch intent where wine is primary

Examples:
- best wineries Mornington Peninsula
- cellar doors Mornington Peninsula
- winery lunch Mornington Peninsula

#### /spa/
Owns:
- spa
- wellness
- hot springs-adjacent planning intent
- day spa and recovery category intent

Examples:
- Mornington Peninsula spa
- day spa Mornington Peninsula
- best spa Mornington Peninsula

#### /dog-friendly/
Owns:
- dog-specific Peninsula travel intent
- dog-friendly beaches / stays / cafés / walks / planning

Examples:
- dog friendly Mornington Peninsula
- dog friendly beaches Mornington Peninsula
- dog friendly accommodation Mornington Peninsula

#### /places/
Owns:
- place and town intent
- cross-category local discovery
- internal authority routing by geography

Examples:
- Red Hill guide
- Sorrento guide
- where to stay in Flinders
- what to do in Mornington

#### /escape/
Owns:
- itinerary-led and trip-shape intent
- couples weekends
- golf weekends
- stay-and-soak packages
- high-utility planning pages

#### /whats-on/
Owns:
- time-sensitive event intent
- this weekend / next weekend / seasonal event planning
- event freshness and recurring local demand

#### /journal/
Owns:
- editorial features
- opinionated service pieces
- seasonal roundups
- commentary and narrative pieces
- supporting authority content

**Important rule:** Journal supports section authority. It should not become the default home for every evergreen head-term target.

## 6. Content Architecture Doctrine

Peninsula Insider should operate with five content layers.

### 6.1 Flagship hubs
These are the category owners.

Examples:
- /explore/
- /stay/
- /eat/
- /wine/
- /spa/
- /dog-friendly/

Job:
- own the broad head term
- set the editorial frame for the category
- route users deeper into the cluster

### 6.2 Support pages
These capture modifiers and intent subsets.

Examples:
- dog-friendly beaches
- spa accommodation
- long lunch wineries
- free things to do
- best towns to base yourself

Job:
- expand long-tail visibility
- strengthen the hub through tighter intent coverage

### 6.3 Place hubs
These connect geography to every relevant category.

Job:
- strengthen local relevance
- create internal-linking structure
- help Google understand place-category relationships

### 6.4 Entity pages
These are the venue / experience / event / accommodation records.

Job:
- create breadth
- support specific search intent
- reinforce place and category authority

### 6.5 Freshness pages
These include What’s On, weekend planning, seasonal packages, and time-sensitive dispatches.

Job:
- increase recrawl cadence
- support repeat visits
- tie organic search to newsletter growth

## 7. Places as the Local Authority Spine

Places are not secondary supporting pages. They are a strategic engine.

Each place page should function as a local discovery hub that links to:
- where to eat there
- where to stay there
- wineries nearby
- things to do there
- events nearby
- relevant Journal pieces
- relevant vertical overlays such as dog-friendly, golf, or spa where relevant

### 7.1 Why this matters
Places do four jobs at once:
1. improve local SEO relevance
2. create powerful internal-linking infrastructure
3. help users navigate naturally by geography
4. connect the editorial and entity layers into one coherent system

### 7.2 Operational rule
Every new content page should ask:
- which place pages should link to this?
- which place pages should this link back to?

If the answer is “none”, the page may be structurally weak.

## 10. Metadata Operating Model

Metadata is not a cleanup layer. It is part of the publishing system.

### 10.1 Required metadata by page type

#### Section hubs
Must include:
- distinct title tag
- clear meta description
- canonical
- Open Graph image and description
- breadcrumb schema
- ItemList or CollectionPage logic where appropriate

#### Journal articles
Must include:
- article title
- meta description
- canonical
- published / modified dates
- Open Graph / Twitter image
- Article schema
- breadcrumb schema

#### Entity pages
Must include:
- entity-first title structure
- category and place context in title/description when useful
- canonical
- Open Graph image
- breadcrumb schema
- type-specific structured data where appropriate

#### Event pages
Must include:
- valid event dates and recurrence handling
- Event schema
- canonical
- updated metadata when dates change

#### Place pages
Must include:
- place-first title structure
- clear editorial description
- breadcrumb schema
- local-cluster internal links

### 10.2 Metadata rules
- no duplicate title tags across major surfaces
- no generic “discover the best of…” descriptions reused sitewide
- no stale dates on time-sensitive pages
- no weak Open Graph defaults on flagship pages
- no missing canonicals

## 11. Technical SEO Governance

### 11.1 Technical priorities
- protocol and canonical consolidation
- trailing slash normalization
- noindex search results pages if needed
- XML sitemap discipline
- breadcrumb consistency
- schema coverage by page type
- freshness signals
- 404 monitoring
- redirect hygiene
- indexation review
- Core Web Vitals review on major templates

### 11.2 Durable rule from recent incidents
Live route status is not enough.
A page is only “healthy” when:
1. the route resolves
2. stylesheet and asset paths resolve
3. visual render is externally confirmed

This should be part of the technical publishing standard going forward.

## 12. Internal Linking Doctrine

Internal linking should be systematic, not ad hoc.

Every page should strengthen the surrounding cluster.

### 12.1 Standard link paths
A support page should link to:
- its parent hub
- relevant place pages
- relevant entity pages
- related Journal or Escape pages

An entity page should link to:
- its category hub
- its place page
- related editorial pieces
- nearby alternatives where relevant

A place page should link to:
- eat highlights
- stay highlights
- explore highlights
- wine highlights if relevant
- current event or seasonal content if relevant

### 12.2 Rule of compounding
A new page should make at least 3–5 existing pages stronger.
If it does not, it may be structurally isolated and therefore weaker than it looks.

## 16. SEO Cron and Recurring Governance Layer

This system should not depend on one-off human attention.
It should run continuously.

The SEO layer should be maintained through a recurring cron structure that audits, identifies gaps, proposes actions, and forces consistency back into the site.

### 16.1 Required recurring SEO jobs

#### pi-weekly-seo-authority-audit
Purpose:
- review section hubs, place hubs, top service pages, and editorial support surfaces
- identify where authority is not compounding properly
- flag thin hubs, weak metadata, weak linking, and ownership drift

#### pi-weekly-seo-opportunity-scan
Purpose:
- scan the site for modifier gaps, cluster gaps, commercial-intent opportunities, and place-led content opportunities
- identify what should be built next rather than relying on intuition

#### pi-weekly-metadata-schema-audit
Purpose:
- verify title tags, descriptions, canonicals, Open Graph coverage, schema output, freshness dates, and page-type metadata consistency

#### pi-weekly-internal-linking-audit
Purpose:
- test whether hubs, places, support pages, and entities are strengthening each other properly
- identify isolated pages and cluster weaknesses

#### pi-monthly-seo-refresh-priority-run
Purpose:
- combine the weekly audit outputs into a concrete refresh and implementation priority queue
- identify which pages need upgrading, consolidating, or reinforcing next

### 16.2 Why cron matters here

These jobs are fundamental because SEO for Peninsula Insider is not static.
The site changes daily through:
- new content
- event turnover
- freshness shifts
- image and metadata updates
- internal-linking changes
- new entities and place pages

Without recurring SEO governance, the site will drift.
With cron, it compounds.

### 16.3 Operational rule
SEO consistency should be treated the same way as:
- accuracy
- image integrity
- broken links
- event freshness

It is an ongoing operational function, not a side project.
