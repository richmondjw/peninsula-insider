# Peninsula Insider — Branding & Creative Cheat Sheet

**Purpose:** Fast execution version of the full Branding & Creative Guide for editors, designers, social producers, newsletter builders, and supporting agents.

Primary reference:
- `docs/peninsula-insider-branding-and-creative-guide-2026-04-20.md`

---

## 1. What Peninsula Insider is

Peninsula Insider is:
- a premium editorial publication
- a trusted local guide
- a selective filter on what is actually worth your time on the Mornington Peninsula

Peninsula Insider is not:
- a tourism board
- a brochure site
- a generic lifestyle publisher
- a directory dump
- a loud promo brand

---

## 2. The core brand line

**The insider guide to the Mornington Peninsula.**

Fallback supporting phrasing:
- Trusted Peninsula picks on food, wine, stays, walks, events, and places
- A local, editorial guide to what is actually worth your time on the Peninsula

---

## 3. Brand test

If a piece is on-brand, it should make people feel:
- these people know the Peninsula
- these people have taste
- these people help me choose well
- this feels better than generic travel content

---

## 4. Tone in one page

### The voice should be
- calm
- informed
- selective
- elegant
- useful
- specific
- lightly opinionated

### The voice should not be
- loud
- gushy
- salesy
- generic
- brochure-like
- over-excited
- empty luxury fluff

### Good writing habits
- name the occasion
- explain who something suits
- frame tradeoffs
- make real recommendations
- use specific nouns
- help people shape a better day or weekend

### Bad writing habits
- saying everything is amazing
- using vague aspiration instead of guidance
- relying on empty superlatives
- sounding like an ad

---

## 5. Words to avoid

Avoid:
- world-class
- hidden gem
- must-visit
- breathtaking
- unforgettable
- pristine beaches
- charming coastal villages
- ultimate guide
- bucket list

These flatten the brand into tourism cliché.

---

## 6. Visual direction

The brand should look like:
- a premium editorial travel publication
- calm, spacious, text-led design
- wine-country restraint, not glossy tourism-board energy
- warm, textured, quietly cinematic Peninsula atmosphere

It should not look like:
- a brochure
- a resort ad
- a flashy luxury campaign
- a busy creator-style content pack
- a generic WordPress template

---

## 7. Colour and typography rules

### Colour world
Use:
- warm cream / parchment
- stone / sand neutrals
- sea-fog blue-grey
- olive / muted eucalyptus
- pinot / oxblood / wine accents
- charcoal / ink neutrals

Avoid:
- loud retail colours
- tropical-bright coastal palettes
- anything that feels promo-heavy

### Typography
Use:
- elegant serif moments for editorial authority
- clean sans serif for readability and interface clarity
- whitespace and scale to create hierarchy

Do not:
- over-style utility text
- create fussy magazine clutter
- trade readability for style theatre

---

## 8. Photography rules

Images should feel:
- real
- local
- atmospheric
- textural
- editorial
- seasonally aware

Images should not feel:
- generic stock travel
- semantically mismatched
- over-saturated tourism-board gloss
- too posed
- too ad-like

### Hard rule
**Images must match the content they are supporting.**
Low-match imagery is off-brand.

### Generative imagery rule
Only use generative images for non-documentary editorial atmosphere.
Never use them as fake evidence of a real place.

---

## 9. Newsletter and email rules

Email should feel like:
- the website in inbox form
- editorial, not promotional sludge
- composed, premium, and useful

Use:
- aligned site palette
- consistent typography
- modular editorial layout
- clean imagery

Avoid:
- marketplace clutter
- growth-hack email styling
- over-designed promo blocks

---

## 10. Social creative rules

Social should feel:
- current
- saveable
- useful
- tasteful
- recognisably Peninsula Insider

Do:
- adapt per platform without breaking the brand
- keep text-on-image minimal unless utility requires it
- make carousels feel editorial, not noisy

Do not:
- overuse stickers, arrows, badges, emoji clutter
- dump links without perspective
- let LinkedIn sound like recycled Instagram copy

---

## 11. Page and article standard

Every major surface should balance:
- inspiration
- selection
- usability
- readability
- trust

A strong Peninsula Insider page should feel:
- editorially shaped
- practically useful
- visually composed
- clearly prioritised

Not like:
- a listing dump
- an SEO page wearing nicer clothes

---

## 12. Approval checklist

Before anything ships, ask:

### Brand fit
- Does this feel like Peninsula Insider?
- Does it feel editorial, selective, and local?

### Voice fit
- Is the wording specific and useful?
- Is there any brochure copy to cut?

### Visual fit
- Does the palette, type, and layout align with the established brand world?

### Image fit
- Does the image genuinely match the subject?
- Is it strong enough for a premium editorial brand?

### Channel fit
- Has it been adapted properly for web, email, or social without losing coherence?

---

## 13. Non-negotiables

- No brochure language.
- No generic tourism-board look.
- No semantically weak imagery.
- No off-brand rush jobs on public surfaces.
- No treating every option as equally worthy.
- No public creative that feels louder, cheaper, or more generic than the site.

---

## 14. Fast reminder

Peninsula Insider should be:
**sharper, calmer, more useful, more trusted, more Peninsula.**
