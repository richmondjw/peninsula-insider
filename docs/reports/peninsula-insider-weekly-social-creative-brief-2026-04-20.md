# Peninsula Insider weekly social creative brief
## For: one week of daily organic posts across Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn
## Date: 2026-04-20

## Recommendation
Run the week visually as **a progression from orientation to decision to lived proof**.

The creative should feel like Peninsula Insider is not shouting for attention, but quietly helping readers picture a better Peninsula week. That means restrained, editorial, place-first visuals with clear utility on Instagram, practical readability on Facebook, and sharper framing on LinkedIn.

## Creative direction for the week
- **Visual centre of gravity:** autumn Peninsula light, coastal restraint, heritage texture, food-and-place specificity, no generic tourism gloss
- **Overall aesthetic:** editorial magazine, not tourism brochure and not creator-style lifestyle fluff
- **People usage:** sparing, observational, and secondary to place unless the human moment is the story
- **Text on image:** minimal, useful, and never relied on as the only communication layer
- **Platform rule:** one core concept per day, then adapt crop, density, and framing by platform rather than building three unrelated ideas

---

## Monday — Set the week's editorial frame
**Editorial role:** orient the audience to what kind of Peninsula week this is

**Visual concept**
A mood-led "week ahead" editorial opener. Use a restrained collage or carousel built from three Peninsula signals: village street texture, cool-season coastline, and one seasonal table or market detail. The feeling should be "this is the shape of the week" rather than "here are events."

**Asset type**
- Instagram: 4–5 slide carousel
- Facebook: single hero image or 2-card carousel
- LinkedIn: single image with strong editorial framing

**Framing**
Lead with place and season. Avoid busy multi-subject layouts. The first image should immediately signal Mornington Peninsula, ideally through a recognisable but non-cliché local cue such as Sorrento village detail, a windswept coastal path, or a market still life.

**Text-on-image guidance**
- Keep to one short line max on opening slide, for example: **This week on the Peninsula**
- No subhead stack, no date clutter, no more than 6 words on cover
- Subsequent slides can use small corner labels only if needed

**Ratio / crop considerations**
- Master in 4:5 for Instagram feed
- Protect centre crop so Facebook can use 1.91:1 without losing the key subject
- Keep LinkedIn-safe version in 1.91:1 with extra negative space for caption-led posting

**Platform variants needed**
- Facebook version should privilege legibility over mood, with slightly simpler composition
- LinkedIn version should feel cleaner and more publication-like, with less collage density

---

## Tuesday — Prove taste with one standout recommendation
**Editorial role:** show selective judgement through one strong local pick

**Visual concept**
A single-subject recommendation post built around **Sorrento as the long-weekend base**. Focus on off-season village or coastline imagery that suggests a smart local choice rather than a crowded attraction.

**Asset type**
- Instagram: 3-slide carousel or single polished hero
- Facebook: single image with link-supportive composition
- LinkedIn: single image

**Framing**
Choose one image with enough authority to hold the post alone: quiet streetscape, heritage facade rhythm, or back beach atmosphere. The image should say, "if you only pick one base, pick this one."

**Text-on-image guidance**
- Optional only
- If used, keep to a simple recommendation line: **Start in Sorrento**
- Avoid badges, arrows, numbered labels, or travel-guide clutter

**Ratio / crop considerations**
- 4:5 master if using carousel
- Also export 1:1 crop for flexibility if the hero is strongly centred
- Facebook and LinkedIn can share a 1.91:1 crop if the subject survives wide framing

**Platform variants needed**
- Instagram can carry a slightly more atmospheric crop
- Facebook should use the clearest place-signalling frame
- LinkedIn should avoid overt consumer-style overlays entirely

---

## Wednesday — Deliver saveable utility
**Editorial role:** help readers make a decision midweek

**Visual concept**
A **mini itinerary / decision-support carousel**. Best expression: a clean three-lane Peninsula day shape such as **walk → lunch → soak** or **culture → village browse → coastal finish**. Use simple editorial cards with restrained photography and clear structure.

**Asset type**
- Instagram: save-first carousel
- Facebook: checklist-style graphic or carousel
- LinkedIn: single summary card or one strong itinerary graphic

**Framing**
This is the week’s most designed utility post. Prioritise clarity, sequence, and saveability. Use one image per stop or one hero plus structured text panels.

**Text-on-image guidance**
- Text is appropriate here
- Keep each panel to one action line only, for example: **Walk at Point Nepean** / **Lunch in Sorrento** / **Late soak at Alba**
- Large enough to read on mobile, but still editorial not infographic-heavy

**Ratio / crop considerations**
- Design primarily in 4:5
- Maintain generous margins so cropped derivatives do not cut text
- For Facebook, consider a simpler 1:1 or wide summary version rather than reposting every IG slide unchanged

**Platform variants needed**
- Instagram gets the full multi-card sequence
- Facebook gets a condensed utility version with fewer panels
- LinkedIn gets a sharper, cleaner single-frame itinerary summary

---

## Thursday — Shape weekend intent
**Editorial role:** narrow options and make choice easier

**Visual concept**
A **choose your Peninsula mood** post with 3 distinct lanes: reflective, indulgent, or family-proof. Each lane should have its own visual cue, but the overall design should stay cohesive and elegant.

**Asset type**
- Instagram: carousel
- Facebook: carousel or static comparison graphic
- LinkedIn: single composed comparison card

**Framing**
Think editorial decision tree, not quiz graphic. Use visual contrast between lanes, for example heritage/culture, thermal/minimal luxury, and indoor family activity, while keeping typography and palette consistent.

**Text-on-image guidance**
- Strong use case for text-on-image
- Cover line can be: **Choose your Peninsula weekend**
- Each lane should have a short label only, ideally 1–3 words
- No emoji, no loud colour coding, no salesy prompts

**Ratio / crop considerations**
- Build master in 4:5
- Ensure each lane remains legible when adapted to 1:1 and 1.91:1
- Avoid putting lane labels too close to the outer edges

**Platform variants needed**
- Facebook can use slightly more explicit labels because it is more practical/planning-led
- LinkedIn should simplify to a more polished split-panel or three-column editorial card

---

## Friday — Convert attention into weekend action
**Editorial role:** become the planning prompt readers act on

**Visual concept**
The flagship **Peninsula This Weekend** asset. This should feel like the most authoritative creative of the week, built around one anchor plan plus two alternates. Recommendation: lead visually with **Sorrento / ANZAC long-weekend base**, supported by Point Nepean and one slower luxury or family fallback lane.

**Asset type**
- Instagram: premium carousel
- Facebook: single hero + link-friendly supporting crop
- LinkedIn: single cover image for editorial note

**Framing**
The opening frame should feel decisive and calm, not overloaded. Use one hero scene that can carry the whole weekend thesis, then follow with alternate plan slides. This is the closest the week gets to a cover story.

**Text-on-image guidance**
- Cover text acceptable and useful here: **This weekend on the Peninsula**
- Optional sub-line only if very short, for example: **Start in Sorrento**
- Keep body detail off the cover and into later carousel panels

**Ratio / crop considerations**
- 4:5 cover master
- Wide 1.91:1 export for Facebook and LinkedIn
- Leave top and bottom breathing room for platform UI overlap and repost flexibility

**Platform variants needed**
- Instagram gets the full editorial package
- Facebook should get a version optimised for immediate practical comprehension
- LinkedIn should use the cleanest single cover with caption carrying the argument

---

## Saturday — Validate the brand through atmosphere
**Editorial role:** show the Peninsula being lived, not merely promoted

**Visual concept**
A light-touch, in-the-moment atmosphere post: sea air, village movement, market table, walking path, or late-afternoon weather texture. It should feel observed, current, and local.

**Asset type**
- Instagram: single image or short story sequence
- Facebook: single image
- LinkedIn: usually no variant unless there is an unusually strong place/economy angle

**Framing**
Favour authenticity over polish. Slight imperfection is fine if the image feels close to the ground and editorially tasteful. Avoid looking like stock.

**Text-on-image guidance**
- Best with no text on image
- If any text is used, keep it tiny and situational, such as **On the Peninsula today**

**Ratio / crop considerations**
- 4:5 or 1:1 works best for immediacy
- Keep subject central enough for reuse in story or square crop

**Platform variants needed**
- Facebook can reuse the Instagram still with minor crop adaptation
- No separate LinkedIn asset unless specifically justified

---

## Sunday — Reflect and renew the habit loop
**Editorial role:** turn the week into memory and set up the next return

**Visual concept**
A quiet recap or reflective close. Best expression is a single strong image or 3-card recap that distils the Peninsula mood of the week, not a cluttered roundup. Think soft afternoon light, end-of-week texture, or one image that feels like an exhale.

**Asset type**
- Instagram: single image or short recap carousel
- Facebook: single image with prompt-led caption
- LinkedIn: optional concise editorial reflection card

**Framing**
This should feel companionable and composed. Less agenda, more editorial afterglow. It is the bridge to next week rather than another conversion push.

**Text-on-image guidance**
- Usually none
- If using text, keep it minimal: **What defined the week** or **See you next week**

**Ratio / crop considerations**
- 4:5 master preferred
- Ensure any recap layout survives 1:1 if reused

**Platform variants needed**
- Facebook may benefit from a slightly warmer, more community-facing crop
- LinkedIn only if the reflection has a clear publishing or regional-intelligence angle

---

## Cross-platform production notes
- **Instagram** should carry the most visual richness and carousel depth
- **Facebook** should use cleaner covers, fewer panels, and stronger immediate readability
- **LinkedIn** should feel like a media brand with taste, not a reposted consumer graphic
- Build from one master concept per day, then adapt crop and text density rather than redesigning from scratch

## Text-on-image rules for the full week
- Use text on image only when it improves decision-making or saveability
- Never exceed one headline and one micro-label on a single frame
- Avoid heavy banners, stickers, drop shadows, or travel-brochure tropes
- Typography should feel like a magazine system: restrained serif/sans pairing or one clean editorial sans

## Image selection guardrails
- No hard-sell tourism imagery
- No oversaturated sunsets unless the story is specifically about dusk/light
- No generic aerials as default solution
- No crowd-heavy summer imagery unless the post is explicitly about an event
- Prefer verifiable Peninsula locations with seasonally believable conditions

## Bottom line
The strongest creative package for the week is one that **looks editorial, feels local, and helps readers choose well**.

If the copy strategy is inspiration → utility → action → proof → renewal, the visual strategy should be:

**place → judgement → structure → decision → atmosphere**.
