# Peninsula Insider — Weekly Social Design Production Brief

**Prepared for:** Design / Frontend Creative Team  
**Prepared by:** Remy  
**Date:** 20 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Turn the approved weekly social strategy, copy package, and creative direction into a production-ready design brief for the creation of social graphics.

---

## 1. Brief objective

Design the full set of weekly social media graphics for Peninsula Insider for the week of **20 to 26 April 2026**.

The graphics should translate the approved editorial strategy into platform-ready visual assets across:
- Instagram
- Facebook
- LinkedIn

This is not a generic social content pack.
It is a branded editorial package.

The job is to create a week of graphics that feels like one coherent Peninsula Insider story unfolding across the week:

> **orientation → taste → utility → decision → action → lived proof → reflection**

The creative must feel like Peninsula Insider is helping readers choose well, not trying to shout for attention.

## 3. What success looks like

A successful delivery will:
- feel clearly and recognisably Peninsula Insider
- look editorial, local, calm, selective, and premium
- avoid tourism-board, influencer, or promo-brand energy
- make the week feel like a coherent narrative, not seven disconnected posts
- be immediately usable by publishing/marketing without redesign
- preserve one master visual idea per day, then adapt it across platforms intelligently

The audience reaction we want is:
- **this looks more considered than normal destination content**
- **this publication has taste**
- **this feels useful, not noisy**
- **I trust this brand to help me choose**

## 4. Brand and creative guardrails

### The graphics must feel
- editorial
- place-first
- refined
- seasonally grounded
- readable
- useful
- premium without flash

### The graphics must not feel
- generic
- brochure-like
- over-designed
- overly promotional
- tourism-board safe
- creator-style cluttered
- glossy ad campaign

### Hard brand rules
- no loud retail/promo colour behaviour
- no stickers, emoji clutter, badges, arrows, or sales graphics
- no generic tourism slogans
- no image choices that do not genuinely match the Peninsula and the post angle
- no text-on-image unless it improves clarity, saveability, or decisional value

---

## 5. Design system direction

### Visual centre of gravity
- autumn Peninsula light
- coastal restraint
- village texture
- heritage detail
- food-and-place specificity
- editorial calm

### Palette direction
Stay within the established Peninsula Insider palette:
- warm cream / parchment
- stone / sand neutrals
- sea-fog blue-grey
- muted olive / eucalyptus
- selective pinot / oxblood accents
- charcoal / ink neutrals

### Typography direction
Use a restrained editorial type system:
- serif moments for authority / headline gravity where appropriate
- clean sans for utility, labels, and clarity

Typography should feel:
- magazine-led
- modern
- premium
- readable

Do not create fussy, layered, decorative layouts.

### Layout direction
Use:
- generous spacing
- clear hierarchy
- one strong focal point per frame
- clean edge discipline
- good negative space

Avoid:
- busy collage overload
- too many messages per frame
- dense infographic behaviour unless the post is explicitly utility-led

---

## 6. Platform production approach

### Rule: one concept per day, adapted across platforms
Do not design three unrelated posts for each day.

Instead:
- create one master editorial concept per day
- adapt crop, density, text treatment, and panel count by platform

### Instagram
Role:
- most visually rich
- strongest carousel depth
- most editorially styled
- saveability matters

### Facebook
Role:
- cleaner, more immediately legible
- stronger practical readability
- fewer panels when possible
- more direct cover-frame comprehension

### LinkedIn
Role:
- publication-grade
- sharp, restrained, intelligent
- less consumer graphic energy
- should feel like a smart media brand, not reposted lifestyle creative

## 8. Daily production brief

## Monday — Set the week’s editorial frame

### Strategic job
Orient the audience to the shape of the Peninsula week ahead.

### Core message
This is the kind of week the Peninsula is having, and Peninsula Insider is the publication helping readers read it properly.

### Visual concept
A restrained mood-led opener using three Peninsula signals:
- village street texture
- cool-season coastline
- seasonal table / market / local detail

This should feel like an editorial opener, not an events flyer.

### Asset structure
- Instagram: 4–5 slide carousel
- Facebook: single hero or 2-card carousel
- LinkedIn: single image

### Text-on-image guidance
- opening line max 6 words
- recommended: **This week on the Peninsula**
- no date clutter
- no subhead stack
- only small secondary labels if truly needed

### Designer note
Think cover-story opener, not “weekly roundup.”
The first frame must immediately feel Peninsula and seasonally grounded.

---

## Tuesday — Prove editorial taste

### Strategic job
Show selective judgement through one strong recommendation.

### Core message
If you only make one smart move this week, start in the right Peninsula pocket.

### Visual concept
Single-subject recommendation built around **Sorrento as the long-weekend base**.
Use a strong place-signalling image such as:
- quiet Sorrento streetscape
- heritage facade rhythm
- coastal edge / back beach atmosphere

### Asset structure
- Instagram: 3-slide carousel or strong single hero
- Facebook: single image
- LinkedIn: single image

### Text-on-image guidance
Optional only.
If used:
- **Start in Sorrento**

No ranking badges, no numbered graphics, no destination-brochure tropes.

### Designer note
This is a taste post, not a utility card.
The image should carry most of the authority.

---

## Wednesday — Deliver saveable utility

### Strategic job
Help readers make a decision and save the post.

### Core message
Here is a better-shaped Peninsula day.

### Visual concept
Mini itinerary / decision-support carousel.
Preferred expression:
- **Walk → Lunch → Soak**

Suggested route logic:
- Point Nepean or equivalent walk
- Sorrento lunch / village browse
- Alba or equivalent late-day unwind

### Asset structure
- Instagram: multi-card saveable carousel
- Facebook: condensed checklist graphic or short carousel
- LinkedIn: single summary itinerary card

### Text-on-image guidance
Text is appropriate here.
Keep each card to one action line.
Examples:
- **Walk at Point Nepean**
- **Lunch in Sorrento**
- **Late soak at Alba**

### Designer note
This is the most “designed” post of the week.
Clarity and saveability matter more than mood density.
Still keep it editorial, not infographic-heavy.

---

## Thursday — Shape weekend intent

### Strategic job
Narrow options and help readers decide what kind of weekend they want.

### Core message
Choose the Peninsula mood that suits the weekend.

### Visual concept
A three-lane **Choose your Peninsula weekend** post.
Suggested lanes:
- Reflective
- Indulgent
- Family-proof

### Asset structure
- Instagram: carousel
- Facebook: static comparison or carousel
- LinkedIn: single polished comparison card

### Text-on-image guidance
Strong use case for text-on-image.
Use:
- **Choose your Peninsula weekend**

Each lane label should be short, ideally 1–3 words.
No colour-coded loudness, no emoji.

### Designer note
Think editorial decision tree, not quiz or infographic.
Elegant contrast between lanes is the goal.

---

## Friday — Convert to action

### Strategic job
Act as the flagship weekly planning prompt.

### Core message
Peninsula This Weekend is the definitive local read for the long weekend.

### Visual concept
This week’s hero asset.
Lead with:
- Sorrento / ANZAC long-weekend base
Support with:
- Point Nepean
- one slower fallback lane
- one indulgent or family-proof alternative

### Asset structure
- Instagram: premium carousel
- Facebook: hero image plus optional supporting asset
- LinkedIn: single clean cover image

### Text-on-image guidance
Recommended cover line:
- **This weekend on the Peninsula**
Optional sub-line:
- **Start in Sorrento**

Keep details off the cover and inside later frames.

### Designer note
This is the most important post of the week.
Treat it like a cover story, not a promo tile.

---

## Saturday — Validate through atmosphere

### Strategic job
Show the Peninsula being lived, not just marketed.

### Core message
The brand understands the Peninsula on the ground, in real time.

### Visual concept
Light-touch atmosphere post.
Suitable subjects:
- market table
- walking path
- sea air / village movement
- cool late-afternoon Peninsula texture

### Asset structure
- Instagram: single image or short story sequence
- Facebook: single image
- LinkedIn: optional only if there is a strong editorial angle

### Text-on-image guidance
Ideally none.
If used, keep tiny and situational:
- **On the Peninsula today**

### Designer note
Slight imperfection is acceptable if it feels observed and real.
Better real and tasteful than polished and generic.

---

## Sunday — Reflect and renew

### Strategic job
Close the week with a companionable reflective note and set up next week’s habit.

### Core message
Peninsula Insider exists to make the Peninsula easier to understand and choose well.

### Visual concept
Quiet reflective close.
Could be:
- single image
- 3-card recap
- warm end-of-week texture

### Asset structure
- Instagram: single image or short recap carousel
- Facebook: single image
- LinkedIn: optional reflection card

### Text-on-image guidance
Usually none.
If used:
- **What defined the week**
- **See you next week**

### Designer note
This should feel like an exhale, not another push.

---

## 9. Image selection guidance

Use imagery that is:
- verifiably Peninsula
- seasonally believable
- calm and editorial
- not crowd-heavy unless the post is event-specific
- not generic aerial-first by default

Prefer:
- recognisable local texture
- shoulder-season realism
- observational place imagery
- images with negative space that support editorial text placement

Avoid:
- oversaturated sunsets unless the light is the actual story
- generic stock beach visuals that could be anywhere
- hard-sell tourism hero clichés
- image reuse that makes the week feel repetitive

---

## 10. Copy handling inside design

Design must support copy, not fight it.

Rules:
- one clear message per frame
- no paragraph blocks on image
- let captions do the heavier explanatory work
- if a frame needs too much copy to work, the concept should be simplified

Priority order:
1. legibility
2. hierarchy
3. mood
4. decorative detail

---

## 11. Production checklist

Before final export, check:

### Brand
- Does this feel recognisably Peninsula Insider?
- Is it calm, selective, editorial, and local?

### Visual quality
- Is the hierarchy clear?
- Is the typography restrained and premium?
- Is the frame uncluttered?

### Strategic fit
- Does the post do the intended job for that day?
- Does it match the approved narrative arc of the week?

### Platform fit
- Has the design been properly adapted for IG / FB / LinkedIn?
- Is Facebook clearer and LinkedIn cleaner where needed?

### Image integrity
- Does the chosen image genuinely suit the post angle?
- Does it avoid generic destination filler?

---

## 12. Final delivery expectation

The design team should deliver a weekly pack that feels like:
- one editorial week
- one coherent brand
- seven distinct moments
- three platforms handled intelligently

The final output should not feel like social media trying to be louder.

It should feel like Peninsula Insider being clearer, calmer, and more useful than everyone else.

---

## 13. Bottom line for designers

Your job is not just to make these posts look good.

Your job is to make them feel like:
- trusted local editorial
- useful visual curation
- branded Peninsula judgement

If the graphics look like a travel brochure, a creator pack, or a generic tourism campaign, they have missed the point.

If they look like a premium local publication helping people choose well, they are right.
