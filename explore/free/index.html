Free Things to Do on the Mornington Peninsula

TL;DR

  • The Peninsula's best free activities are beaches, coastal walks, and lookouts — all requiring nothing but a car and a park.
  • Cape Schanck boardwalk is free. The lighthouse precinct entry fee is separate and optional.
  • Point Nepean National Park requires a Parks Victoria entry fee — not free.
  • Mornington Peninsula National Park foreshore access is largely free.

The Mornington Peninsula, Victoria, Australia has a meaningful free-activities menu. Beaches are free. Most foreshore walks are free. Lookouts are free. Markets are free to browse. The paid attractions — Peninsula Hot Springs, Arthurs Seat Eagle, Ashcombe Maze, Point Nepean — are all optional additions to what's already here.

Beaches — all free

Every beach on the Peninsula is free to access. Parking may charge fees in some locations but the beach itself is public.

  • Balnarring Beach — Bay side. Free parking. Best family beach on the Peninsula.
  • Mount Martha Beach — Bay side. Foreshore strip, good walking.
  • Sorrento Back Beach — Ocean side. One of the best free coastal experiences in Victoria.
  • Gunnamatta Ocean Beach — Ocean side. Remote, powerful. No charge.
  • Safety Beach foreshore — Bay side. Long foreshore. Free.

Walks — most are free

Fully free:

  • Cape Schanck boardwalk — The boardwalk itself is free. The lighthouse precinct charges a separate entry fee (optional).
  • Bushrangers Bay walk — Free. One of the Peninsula's best walks.
  • Mornington foreshore walk — Free. Flat and accessible.
  • Coppins Track, Sorrento — Free access to the track. Check current national park rules.
  • Greens Bush section, Two Bays Walking Track — Free.

Requires entry fee:

  • Point Nepean National Park — Parks Victoria fee applies.
  • Cape Schanck lighthouse precinct — Entry fee if you want to visit the lighthouse itself.

Lookouts — all free

  • Arthurs Seat State Park summit — Drive to the summit car park (free). The gondola is a separate paid experience. The views from the summit car park itself are excellent and free.
  • Cape Schanck clifftop area — Significant views accessible without paying the lighthouse entry.
  • Flinders cliff walk — The headland above Flinders pier has cliff-top views over Bass Strait with no fee.

Markets — free to browse

  • Red Hill Community Market — Free entry. First Saturdays.
  • Balnarring Farmers Market — Free entry. Third Saturdays.
  • Rye Beachside Market — Free entry.

Foreshore activities

The Peninsula's bay-side foreshore from Mornington to Sorrento is public land. The Mornington foreshore strip, the Safety Beach to Dromana path, and the Sorrento bay side are all free to use.

FAQ

What can you do on the Mornington Peninsula for free?

Most beaches are free. The national park foreshore walks are largely free. Cape Schanck boardwalk is free; the lighthouse precinct charges a separate entry fee. Arthurs Seat summit car park is free; the gondola is paid. All markets are free to browse.

Is Cape Schanck boardwalk free?

The boardwalk itself is free. The Cape Schanck lighthouse precinct charges a Parks Victoria entry fee if you want to visit the lighthouse. The boardwalk walk to the basalt wave platform is accessible without paying the lighthouse fee.

Is Point Nepean National Park free?

No. Point Nepean National Park requires a Parks Victoria entry fee. Most of the rest of the Mornington Peninsula National Park (foreshore access, clifftop walks) is free.
