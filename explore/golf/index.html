Mornington Peninsula Golf

TL;DR

  • The Peninsula has 11 golf courses in this guide — ranging from sandbelt-adjacent private clubs to accessible public links.
  • Moonah Links and The National are the two marquee public-access courses; side by side at Cape Schanck.
  • St Andrews Beach is the best value experience of the three Cape Schanck courses.
  • All courses in this guide accept green-fee visitors unless marked Private.

The Mornington Peninsula, Victoria, Australia has one of the most concentrated collections of notable golf courses of any region in Australia. The southern end — around Cape Schanck and Fingal — has three world-ranked courses within 10 minutes of each other. The bay-side corridor from Mornington to Sorrento has a further run of accessible public and semi-private clubs. This hub ranks 11 courses in editorial order — not by prestige rankings but by what a visiting golfer actually gets for their money and time.

The 11 courses ranked

The Cape Schanck / Fingal group

1. Moonah Links — Fingal

Access: Public green fees. Format: 36 holes (Open Course + Legends Course). Difficulty: Challenging.

The Peninsula's most consistently excellent public-access golf. The Open Course is a Peter Thomson/Ross Perrett sandbelt design that has hosted the Australian Open. Strong ocean views on several holes. Better value than The National for mid-handicappers who want a great round rather than a punishment. The Legends Course is shorter, more accessible, and worth playing if you want a second round without the Open Course's exposure.

Best for: Mid-handicap players wanting a top-tier round. Anyone hosting a group.

2. The National Golf Club — Cape Schanck

Access: Public green fees (Ocean Course). Private for members (Moonah/Gunnamatta courses).

The National's Ocean Course is the most-discussed and most-photographed course on the Peninsula. Clifftop holes over Bass Strait. Regularly listed among Australia's top ten courses. Expensive. Exposed and difficult in wind. For a single-digit handicapper, it delivers. For mid-handicappers who need to focus on the round rather than the scenery, Moonah Links is a better experience.

Best for: Low-handicap players seeking a bucket-list round. Groups who want the "best on the Peninsula" experience.

3. St Andrews Beach Golf Course — Fingal

Access: Public green fees. Format: 18 holes.

Often overlooked between its famous neighbours, St Andrews Beach is a genuinely enjoyable course with good ocean views, less exposure than The National, and a substantially lower green fee. The clubhouse experience is relaxed. Best value of the three Cape Schanck courses.

Best for: Those who want Cape Schanck golf without the premium price or the difficulty.

4. RACV Cape Schanck Resort Golf Course — Cape Schanck

Access: Public green fees; resort guests. Format: 18 holes.

A Graham Marsh resort design. Not at the level of Moonah or National in prestige but a solid resort round, and the only option in this cluster that integrates directly with accommodation and spa. Good for golf-and-wellness trips.

Best for: Resort stays. Golf + spa combination days. Accessible difficulty.

Bay corridor clubs (Mornington to Sorrento)

5. Flinders Golf Club — Flinders

Access: Public green fees. Format: 18 holes.

Cliff-top and paddock golf with Bass Strait views on several holes. The club punches above its tier in scenery. A genuine hidden-gem course that most Melbourne golfers haven't played. The town of Flinders adds a worthwhile food-and-drink stop afterward.

Best for: Those based in the south; golfers who want ocean views without Cape Schanck prices.

6. Eagle Ridge Golf Course — Merricks North

Access: Public green fees. Format: 18 holes.

Hinterland setting, rolling terrain. Not the ocean drama of the Cape courses but a well-regarded public course in the hinterland corridor. Near several Red Hill wineries for a natural pairing.

Best for: Hinterland-based visits; combining with a winery lunch.

7. Mornington Golf Club — Mornington

Access: Public green fees. Format: 18 holes.

Bay-side position, accessible pricing, and a comfortable clubhouse. The Peninsula's most approachable layout for high-handicappers and occasional golfers. Mornington town is immediately adjacent.

Best for: High-handicappers. Social golf. A town day that includes a round.

8. The Dunes Golf Links — Rye

Access: Public green fees. Format: 18 holes.

Links-style layout in the dune country near Rye. Less dramatic than the Cape Schanck group but a genuine links feel and affordable. Exposed to wind — plays completely differently in a south-westerly.

Best for: Links enthusiasts. Midweek green fees at accessible pricing.

9. Sorrento Golf Club — Sorrento

Access: Members and guest days. Visitor green fees available but check availability. Format: 18 holes.

One of the Peninsula's older clubs, in a clifftop setting at the tip. Semi-private with visitor access on non-competition days. Call ahead. The position is outstanding.

Best for: Those staying in Sorrento who want a local round.

10. Portsea Golf Club — Portsea

Access: Members and reciprocal. Limited visitor access.

Largely private. The course occupies part of the Point Nepean headland. Outstanding natural setting; restricted access.

Best for: Members and reciprocal club visitors only.

11. Rosebud Country Club — Rosebud

Access: Public green fees. Format: 18 holes.

The most accessible public course in the mid-Peninsula. Flat, straightforward, and affordable. Not a destination round but a convenient option for those based in the Rosebud/Rye corridor.

Best for: Convenience; high-handicappers; family groups.

FAQ

What is the best golf course on the Mornington Peninsula?

The National's Ocean Course is the most cited for prestige. Moonah Links' Open Course delivers better value and a more enjoyable round for most visiting golfers.

Can you play golf on the Mornington Peninsula without a club membership?

Yes. Moonah Links, The National (Ocean Course), St Andrews Beach, RACV Cape Schanck, Flinders, Mornington, The Dunes, and Rosebud Country Club all accept visitor green fees.

How much does golf cost on the Mornington Peninsula?

Rates vary significantly. Expect to pay a premium at The National and Moonah Links. St Andrews Beach, Mornington, Rosebud, and The Dunes are more accessible.

Is there accommodation near the Peninsula golf courses?

RACV Cape Schanck Resort is on-site. Moonah Links has nearby accommodation.
