Mornington Peninsula Hot Springs

TL;DR

  • There are two thermal springs operators on the Mornington Peninsula: Peninsula Hot Springs at Fingal and Alba Thermal Springs at Rye.
  • Peninsula Hot Springs is larger, older, more facilities — and more crowded. Book 1–2 weeks ahead on weekends, year-round.
  • Alba is newer, smaller, quieter, and easier to book. It suits visitors who find Peninsula Hot Springs too busy.
  • Both operate in all weather. Wet and cold days are, counter-intuitively, the best time to visit.
  • See the direct comparison at Peninsula Hot Springs vs Alba.

The Mornington Peninsula, Victoria, Australia sits above a geothermal aquifer that the Peninsula Hot Springs complex has been tapping since 2005. The thermal springs have become the Peninsula's most-booked paid experience — and the opening of Alba Thermal Springs at Rye gave visitors a genuine alternative for the first time.

Peninsula Hot Springs

Location: Springs Lane, Fingal, VIC 3939. Getting there: 20 km south of Dromana via Boneo Road. Car access only — no public transport to the site.

Peninsula Hot Springs is the Peninsula's flagship thermal experience. The site covers a hillside at Fingal with over 60 bathing experiences — pools at different temperatures, a bathhouse, a spa facility, a day spa, glamping accommodation, and a café. The scale and range of experience types are genuinely unique in Victoria.

What to expect: Bathing terraces built into a hillside, each pool at a different temperature (typically 38–42°C for the main thermal pools). The hilltop pool has the best views. The cave pool is popular and often crowded. Crowds are the main complaint — Saturday mornings in summer can feel less like relaxation and more like managing queues between pools.

Best time to visit:

  • Midweek: Noticeably quieter. Monday–Thursday.
  • Winter: The contrast between cold air and hot water is the whole point. Also the most atmospheric. Book a midweek slot in July for the Peninsula's peak immersive experience.
  • Early morning or evening session: Avoid the 11am–3pm peak window.
  • Avoid: Saturday afternoons in summer. Long queues between pools are the rule not the exception.

Alba Thermal Springs

Location: Rye, VIC. Getting there: within Rye township, accessible from the Nepean Highway.

Alba opened after Peninsula Hot Springs and positioned itself deliberately as a quieter, more design-focused alternative. Fewer pools — and fewer people. The site is smaller, which is its primary differentiator: you are unlikely to feel crowded at Alba on a midweek visit, and even peak weekends are more manageable than Peninsula Hot Springs.

Less variety than Peninsula Hot Springs; more consistency in the quality of each experience. The design aesthetic is considered where Peninsula Hot Springs has grown organically.

Deciding between them

See the full editorial analysis at Peninsula Hot Springs vs Alba. In brief:

Peninsula Hot SpringsAlba
ScaleLarge (60+ pools)Small (curated selection)
Crowd levelHigh on weekendsLower, even on weekends
Booking difficultyHard (weekends)Easier

Best forFirst-timers; maximum varietyReturn visitors; those who want peace

Peninsula Hot Springs is the place to understand what the fuss is about. Alba is the place to return to once you've ticked the main experience.

What to pair with a hot springs visit

  • Walk + soak: Cape Schanck boardwalk or Bushrangers Bay walk before an afternoon soak (both within 15–20 minutes by car).
  • Dinner + soak: A midday soak followed by dinner at Fingal or in Rye.
  • Spa + springs: Book a treatment at a Peninsula day spa on the same day.

FAQ

Do I need to book Peninsula Hot Springs in advance?

Yes. Same-day booking on weekends is almost impossible in peak season (December–February, July school holidays). Book 1–2 weeks ahead for weekends. Midweek is easier.

Can you visit Peninsula Hot Springs without booking ahead?

Occasionally midweek in low season, but the risk of turning up to a full site is real. The website shows real-time availability.

What is the difference between Peninsula Hot Springs and Alba?

Peninsula Hot Springs is larger, older, and has more pool variety but is significantly busier on weekends. Alba is smaller, quieter, and easier to book. Full comparison: Peninsula Hot Springs vs Alba.

Are hot springs suitable in summer?

Yes. Many pools are at 38–40°C year-round. Winter soaks in the open air are uniquely atmospheric, but the springs operate year-round.

Is there anything else near Peninsula Hot Springs to visit on the same day?

Cape Schanck (15 minutes south) for a walk before the soak. Rye Ocean Beach (15 minutes north) for a surf-and-soak combination.
