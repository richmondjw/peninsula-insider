Mornington Peninsula Markets

TL;DR

  • Five main markets: Red Hill Community Market, Mornington Main Street Market, Rye Beachside Market, Balnarring Farmers Market, and Mount Eliza Farmers Market.
  • Red Hill Market (first Saturday of the month) is the Peninsula's most complete market — best produce, best food stalls, best artisan work.
  • Balnarring Farmers Market runs on the third Saturday; more food-producer focused.
  • All markets are outdoor and weather-dependent, though most run in light rain.
  • Early arrival matters — Red Hill is worth being there by 8:30am in summer.

The best Peninsula markets are not craft-fair exercises — they are genuine producers' markets, strong on fruit, vegetables, meat, cheese, prepared food, and (at Red Hill especially) the kind of food-producer conversation you don't get at a supermarket.

The five markets ranked

1. Red Hill Community Market — Red Hill

When: First Saturday of every month, 8am–1pm. Where: Red Hill Showgrounds, Arthurs Seat Road, Red Hill.

The anchor of the Peninsula's market calendar. Strong produce from hinterland growers. Prepared food from quality vendors — fresh pastries, wood-fired bread, local cheese. Artisan stalls that lean toward quality craft rather than generic souvenir ware. The atmosphere at 8:30am on a clear autumn morning is one of the Peninsula's unrepeatable small pleasures.

Arrive early in summer (before 9am) or accept that the carpark is full and the best produce vendors have sold out. The market feeds directly into how to build a Red Hill Saturday — a hinterland loop that uses the market as its anchor.

Best for: Produce. Artisan food. A hinterland Saturday morning.

2. Balnarring Farmers Market — Balnarring

When: Third Saturday of every month, 8am–12:30pm. Where: Balnarring Racecourse, Coolart Road, Balnarring.

Smaller than Red Hill Market, more food-producer focused, minimal craft. The producers are largely from the Western Port hinterland — beef, lamb, eggs, honey, seasonal produce. The setting at the racecourse reserve is genuinely pastoral. The Merricks General Wine Store is 5 km away for a natural continuation.

Best for: Serious food producers. A quieter market experience. Combining with the Merricks/Shoreham corridor.

3. Mornington Main Street Market — Mornington

When: Second and fourth Wednesdays, plus some Saturdays. Where: Main Street, Mornington.

The town market for the northern Peninsula. More accessibility-focused — some craft, some produce, some street food. Good for combining with a Mornington town visit. Less essential if you're already doing Red Hill.

Best for: Midweek visits. Town-day additions. Northern Peninsula stays.

4. Rye Beachside Market — Rye

A beachside market on the bay foreshore. Largely crafts and gift items with some food. Lower food-producer density than Red Hill or Balnarring. The setting — beachside in summer — is the draw more than the stalls. Good for a casual browse on a Sunday morning if you're already in Rye.

Best for: Casual atmosphere. A Rye Sunday morning add-on.

5. Mount Eliza Farmers Market — Mount Eliza

A smaller community market in the northern Peninsula town of Mount Eliza. Good local produce from the Mornington corridor. Lower volume than Red Hill but worthwhile for those based in the northern area.

Best for: Northern Peninsula visitors. Combining with a Mount Eliza coffee stop.

How to combine markets with a Peninsula day

The Red Hill and Balnarring markets are two Saturdays apart. A weekend itinerary built around both is practical:

  • Saturday 1 (Red Hill Saturday): Market in the morning, winery lunch in the afternoon. See how to build a Red Hill Saturday.
  • Saturday 3 (Balnarring Saturday): Market in the morning, then Shoreham or the Merricks General Wine Store in the afternoon.

FAQ

When is the Red Hill Market?

The first Saturday of every month, 8am–1pm, at Red Hill Showgrounds.

What is the best market on the Mornington Peninsula?

Red Hill Community Market — for variety, quality, and atmosphere. Balnarring Farmers Market if you prioritise food producers over craft and artisan goods.

Are there markets on the Mornington Peninsula every week?

No. The flagship markets (Red Hill, Balnarring) are monthly. Mornington Main Street Market runs fortnightly. There is no significant weekly market.

Are the markets suitable for visiting in winter?

Yes. Red Hill and Balnarring markets run year-round. Winter attendance is lower, which many visitors prefer. Wet weather can affect stall numbers at open-air markets.

Red Hill Community Market

The Red Hill Community Market is still the cleanest expression of upper-Peninsula weekend culture: growers, pantry stalls, ceramics, linen, very good coffee, and enough local texture to make the whole region feel less polished and more inhabited. It works best when treated as a beginning rather than the main event. Arrive early, move quickly through the first pass, then take your produce and appetite into the rest of the day. If you are building a Saturday around Red Hill, this is where to start.
