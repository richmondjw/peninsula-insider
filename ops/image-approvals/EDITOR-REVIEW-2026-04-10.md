## 🔑 Three Gating Decisions (Resolve These First)

### Decision 1: Accept CC-BY-SA on images?

**The question:** Can Peninsula Insider use CC-BY-SA images?

**What it means:** Share-alike applies to the *image file and its derivatives*, not to the HTML page or site CSS. Using a CC-BY-SA photo on a commercial website is standard practice (Wikipedia, news sites, tourism boards all do this). The SA obligation means if someone crops or colour-grades the image and redistributes *the image*, they must keep the same licence. It does NOT make your website open-source.

**Impact:** YES unlocks 7 additional slots immediately (home cover, places hub, explore hub, Cape Schanck, Flinders, Point Nepean, late-afternoon-walks journal). NO defers all 7 until Visit Victoria Content Hub registration or venue media kits arrive.

**Recommendation:** Accept SA. It's industry-standard and unlocks 70% of the ready pipeline.

> **YOUR CALL:** `[ ] Accept CC-BY-SA for Tier A images` or `[ ] Reject — defer SA slots`
