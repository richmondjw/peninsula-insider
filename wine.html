Cellar Doors & Wineries

Wine Country

The Mornington Peninsula is one of Australia's most exciting wine regions — not for volume, but for quality and character. The cool maritime climate produces Chardonnay and Pinot Noir of genuine elegance. Most estates are small, family-run, and proud of it. This is wine country where the winemaker might pour your tasting themselves.

Insider

The general rule on the peninsula: don't try to visit more than three cellar doors in a day, or you'll rush through all of them and remember none. Pick two or three, arrive relaxed, and let the conversation happen. The best cellar door experiences feel more like visiting a friend who happens to make excellent wine.

Ten Minutes by Tractor
Main Ridge
★★★★★

Three separate vineyards — Wallis, McCutcheon and Judd — each produce distinct single-vineyard expressions. The cellar door experience is one of the most complete on the peninsula. Exceptional Chardonnay and Pinot Noir. Try the Wallis Chardonnay if you can.

Premium · Single Vineyard

Must Visit
Pt Leo Estate
Merricks
★★★★★

More than just a winery — a 16-acre outdoor sculpture park featuring works from over 60 Australian and international artists, plus the fine-dining Laura restaurant. The estate Chardonnay, Pinot Gris, Pinot Noir and Shiraz are all excellent.

Sculpture Park · Full Experience
🍷
Old Vines
Paringa Estate
Red Hill South

Some of the peninsula's oldest vines and a winemaking philosophy that prioritises restraint and complexity. The estate Pinot Noir is benchmark peninsula wine — structured, long, genuinely exciting. One of the most important estates in the region.

Old Vines · Benchmark Pinot
🌿
Gardens
Montalto
Red Hill South

Seven vineyards spread across what was supposed to be a retirement project. The gardens and sculpture trail are as impressive as the wines. Walk the grounds, taste the single-vineyard Chardonnays, and stay for lunch.

Gardens · Seven Vineyards
🍶
Natural · Unusual
Quealy Winemakers
Balnarring

An intimate, lo-fi cellar door run by two peninsula pioneers who were experimenting with skin-contact wines and obscure Italian varieties long before anyone else. Brilliant Pinot Grigio, Friulano, Malvasia. One of the most interesting tastings in the region.

Natural · Italian Varieties
🥂
Single Vineyard
Yabby Lake
Tuerong

Since 1998, Yabby Lake has built a reputation for outstanding single-vineyard and single-block expressions. The Chardonnay here is among the finest on the peninsula — precise, textural, and genuinely exciting.

Outstanding Chardonnay
🍷
Pinot Grigio Pioneer
T'Gallant Vineyard
Main Ridge

Known for its Pinot Grigio (T'Gallant essentially pioneered the variety in Australia), this is one of the most accessible and family-friendly winery experiences on the peninsula. Italian-style dining, large shaded outdoor space, real character.

Family Friendly
🌾
Historic
Moorooduc Estate
Moorooduc

One of the oldest vineyards on the peninsula. Owner Richard McIntyre is genuinely one of the country's elder statesmen of low-intervention wine philosophy. Excellent Chardonnay and Pinot Noir. The peninsula's wine scene has been serious for a very long time.

Historic · Low-intervention
Insider

The Red Hill Hinterland Triangle — Paringa, Montalto, and Polperro — are all within a few minutes of each other and make an ideal afternoon loop. Park the car at one, do a proper tasting, have lunch, then walk or drive to the next. Three hours, three perspectives on Red Hill Pinot Noir.
