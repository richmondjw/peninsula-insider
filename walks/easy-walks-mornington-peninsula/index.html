Easy Walks

Easy Walks on the Mornington Peninsula

Not all easy walks are the same. The Cape Schanck boardwalk, the Mornington foreshore, and the first section of Coppins Track are all technically "easy" — but they serve completely different days. This guide matches you to the right easy walk rather than just ranking them.

Easy walks · Cape Schanck · Mornington foreshore

What "easy" actually means here

Flat, short, and genuinely good — all three matter.

"Easy walk" is one of the most abused phrases in Peninsula tourism. It has been used to describe walks that are perfectly fine for a fit adult but genuinely not appropriate for a family with a two-year-old, or for someone who gets anxious on exposed clifftops. We use it specifically: flat or near-flat, under 2 hours return, no scrambling, and suitable for most adults without walking experience. Where a walk has conditions, we say so.

Dogs are banned from Mornington Peninsula National Park and Greens Bush. Easy walk or not, that is a hard rule and it eliminates most of the Peninsula's best short tracks. The honest sections below say so clearly.

The walks

Six easy walks worth making the drive for

Each one is genuinely easy by any reasonable definition. Each one is also genuinely good — not a consolation prize for people who don't want a proper hike.

20 min return · Accessible No dogs

Cape Schanck Boardwalk

20 minutes return from the Cape Schanck carpark to the lighthouse platform above Bass Strait. The boardwalk is surfaced and accessible for most mobility levels. The view from the platform — lighthouse, ocean, and the basalt stack called Pulpit Rock — is one of the strongest short-walk payoffs on the Peninsula. Do not skip it because it is short. Best time: any daylight, but late afternoon light is exceptional. Dogs not permitted in the national park.

3km one way · Flat Dogs on lead OK

Mornington Foreshore Walk

The flat path between Mornington Pier and Schnapper Point, passing the bathing boxes and foreshore reserve. 3km one way, easily shortened. No elevation. Cafés at both ends. The best family or low-effort walk in the northern Peninsula corridor — not spectacular, but reliably good and works in any weather or fitness level. Dogs on lead are permitted on most foreshore sections. Best time: early morning or dusk for the bathing boxes light.

1.5km one way · Clifftop exposure No dogs

Farnsworth Track

1.5km one way from London Bridge carpark to the Portsea Ocean Beach lookout. Technically easy — the track is clear and not steep — but it has genuine clifftop exposure above Bass Strait that may not suit everyone. The reward is the best lookout above the Southern Ocean near Sorrento. Best time: late afternoon (4–5pm) for the light on the cliffs. Dogs not permitted. Not suitable for people uncomfortable near cliffs.

1.8km circuit · Some steps No dogs

Arthurs Seat Summit Circuit

1.8km circuit from the Arthurs Seat summit — the TC McKeller Walk. Formed path with some steps, well-maintained, shaded by messmate and manna gum. Views to Melbourne, the You Yangs, and Mt Macedon on a clear day. Can be combined with the Arthurs Seat Eagle gondola for a one-way walk down. Best time: clear mornings in autumn and winter for the furthest views. Dogs not permitted in the State Park.

1.6km circuit · 45 min No dogs

Baldrys Short Circuit, Greens Bush

The entry-level walk into Victoria's largest surviving coastal heathland remnant. 1.6km, 45 minutes, easy grades, ancient grasstrees. Quiet on weekdays. The bush here feels wilder than the distance from Melbourne suggests — nothing on the Peninsula looks like Greens Bush in the early morning. Best time: dawn or dusk for wildlife (kangaroos, echidnas, wallabies). Dogs not permitted.

Open distance · Flat clifftop No dogs

Coppins Track — first section

The full Coppins Track from Sorrento Ocean Beach to Diamond Bay is 4km one way and moderate. The first section from Sorrento is easy — flat clifftop path above Bass Strait, you walk as far as you want and turn back. Broad views, no scrambling, clear path. A strong option for anyone who wants a clifftop walk without committing to the full route. Best time: morning before the wind picks up. Dogs not permitted in the national park.

Walk selector

Which easy walk belongs to your day?

Easy walks are still part of a Peninsula day. Match the walk to what comes before and after it.

If you want spectacular without effort

Cape Schanck boardwalk. 20 minutes. Lighthouse, Bass Strait, Pulpit Rock. The most impressive short walk on the Peninsula by any measure. Combine with Bushrangers Bay (5km return) if the group wants more after.

If you're bringing small children

Mornington foreshore. Flat, café at both ends, bathing boxes for photographs, and the children can play on the beach after. The Cape Schanck boardwalk also works for prams as far as the lighthouse platform — check current access conditions.

If you want a walk before lunch in Sorrento

Coppins Track first section. Start at Sorrento Ocean Beach, walk the clifftop as far as you like, return, and walk into town for lunch. Earns the meal without requiring commitment to the full route.

If you want views + wine in one morning

Arthurs Seat summit circuit, then Red Hill cellar doors. 1.8km at the summit, then drop into the Red Hill plateau for the afternoon. The gondola gives you an easy way up or down without the drive.

If you want something genuinely different

Baldrys Short Circuit in Greens Bush. Ancient grasstrees, real bush, wildlife if you are there at the right time of day. Nothing looks like Greens Bush anywhere else on the Peninsula. Quiet on weekdays.

If you want drama with a short time window

Farnsworth Track at 4pm. 1.5km to a clifftop lookout above Bass Strait. Do it in the last two hours of daylight — the light on the cliffs and the Southern Ocean at that hour is the best thing you can do on the Peninsula in 30 minutes.

After the walk

What to do when the walk ends

Easy walks are short enough to fit into a larger Peninsula day. These are the natural second acts.

After Cape Schanck

Peninsula Hot Springs — 25 minutes north via the Cape Schanck Road. The walk-then-springs day is the strongest combination on the southern Peninsula. Book the springs session in advance. → Hot springs guide

After Mornington foreshore

Main Street lunch — walk directly from the foreshore into Mornington town. Multiple good brunch and lunch spots. The Wednesday farmers' market is worth timing the walk around if you can. → Mornington guide

After Farnsworth Track or Coppins Track

Sorrento lunch — 10 minutes from the Sorrento end of Coppins Track. Several good restaurants and cafés on Ocean Beach Road and the main street. → Restaurant guide

After Arthurs Seat

Red Hill cellar doors — the plateau is right there. Montalto, Polperro, or any of a dozen producers within 15 minutes. The best walk-then-wine sequence on the Peninsula. → Cellar door guide

Dogs and easy walks

The honest guide to dog access

Mornington Peninsula National Park — which includes Cape Schanck, the Farnsworth Track, the Coppins Track, Two Bays sections, Point Nepean, and the Coastal Walk corridor — prohibits dogs entirely. This includes carparks. There are no exceptions for small dogs, for leads, or for national park boundaries you cannot see from the trailhead.

Greens Bush and Arthurs Seat State Park also prohibit dogs.

For dogs, the clearest options are: the Mornington foreshore (on lead, check local council rules for specific sections), Mount Martha clifftop sections (on lead), and Rosebud foreshore. These are genuinely good walks with dogs. The clifftop national park walks are not available to you. Plan accordingly.

Common questions

What walkers ask about easy Peninsula walks

Is the Cape Schanck boardwalk wheelchair accessible?

The boardwalk is surfaced and largely accessible, but it is not flat throughout and some sections have inclines. Parks Victoria lists it as accessible with assistance. Call 13 1963 before visiting if accessibility is a primary concern. Current upgrade works (as of April 2026) may affect sections — check the Parks Victoria website for current conditions.

Can I do an easy Peninsula walk in winter?

Yes — winter is underrated for short walks. The Cape Schanck boardwalk in a moderate swell is dramatically better than the same walk in flat summer conditions. Greens Bush is quiet and atmospheric in winter. Bring a waterproof layer; the weather can change quickly on exposed clifftop sections. Mud on bush tracks after rain is the main practical consideration.

How far is Cape Schanck from Melbourne?

About 90 minutes from Melbourne CBD via the Mornington Peninsula Freeway and Cape Schanck Road. Worth it for a day trip, especially paired with a stop at Peninsula Hot Springs on the return drive. Drive time varies significantly on peak summer weekends — allow 30 minutes extra on Saturdays in January and February.
