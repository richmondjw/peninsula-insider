◆ The Insider’s First Secret

The 3pm cellar door rule

Most visitors arrive at Peninsula cellar doors between 11am and 1pm. The tasting room is staffed, the script is polished, and you get the same pour as everyone else.

Arrive at 3pm on a Saturday and the room is almost empty. At most Red Hill and Main Ridge producers, the afternoon shift is when the winemaker comes out from the back. You stop getting the tasting menu and start getting a conversation. The wines don’t change. The experience does.

This works especially well at Eldridge Estate, Dexter Wines, and Paringa Estate — the three producers where the 3pm window most reliably gets you the person who actually made the wine.
